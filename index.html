When to wash: Your hair never feels oily — wash when it feels heavy/stiff from product buildup, when frizz gets unmanageable, or when you want to restyle. Every 3-4 days is a good baseline.

Humidity check

Dry (<40% RH) Glycerin in NYM gel is fine. Focus on sealing moisture in. 21-in-1 spray is your friend.
Moderate (40-70%) Everything works. Best styling conditions. NYM gel performs optimally here.
Humid (>70%) NYM gel's glycerin can pull excess moisture in. If frizz persists despite gel, switch to Got2b Ultra Glued (same PQ-69, no glycerin). Marc Anthony spray is essential for blowouts.

Why am I frizzy?

No humidity barrier You styled without gel (curly) or blowout spray (heat). Without a film-forming polymer, humidity enters the cuticle and breaks hydrogen bonds. Fix: always finish with NYM gel or Marc Anthony spray.
Cuticle still weathered Your 18-MEA lipid layer is gone on mid-lengths/ends. Amodimethicone (Garnier conditioner) replaces it over time. Keep using it consistently — cumulative benefit over 2-4 weeks.
Overnight friction Cotton pillowcase disrupts curl clumps and lifts cuticle. Silk pillowcase reduces this. For curly days: pineapple (loose high ponytail) on silk.
TE regrowth halo Short regrowing hairs at hairline/temples. Too short to lie flat — time is the fix (12-18 months to blend). Manage: smooth with a tiny bit of gel in the direction you want them to go.
Hair sticking out at angles Coarse hair sets in whatever position it dries in. Without hold product + directional drying, strands go wherever they want. Fix: gel (curly) or blowout spray + FlexStyle with intention (heat). Small sections, direct each one.
Product buildup Hair feels heavy, coated, products sitting on top instead of absorbing. Time to clarify (EverPure Clarifying shampoo + scalp brush).

Science Notes

Amodimethicone

Amino-modified silicone that selectively binds to damaged (negatively charged) cuticle sites via its positive amine groups. Self-limiting — deposits where needed, stops where hair is healthy. Won't build up like regular dimethicone. Cumulative benefit over weeks. Your best conditioners all have this as a primary ingredient.

Ceramide R (2-Oleamido-1,3-Octadecanediol)

Synthetic lipid that mimics the 18-MEA layer — the natural waxy coating on virgin hair that makes it feel silky. Once damaged (by heat, UV, weathering), 18-MEA cannot regenerate. This ceramide physically replaces it. Found in Garnier Color Repair conditioner. L'Oréal patented technology trickling down from Kérastase.

PQ-69 (NYM Gel)

Polyquaternium-69. Forms a rigid film that maintains structural integrity in moisture. Specifically engineered for humidity resistance — blocks atmospheric water vapor from entering the hair shaft and breaking hydrogen bonds. Dries weightless after scrunching out the cast.

Polysilicone-29 (Marc Anthony Spray)

Heat-activated cross-linking polymer. Under heat + tension (FlexStyle), it forms a hydrophobic (water-repelling) matrix over the cuticle. Water beads up and rolls off. Lasts 3+ washes. Needs clarifying to remove. Blocks other products from absorbing while active — do treatments BEFORE applying, not after.

Heat Damage Threshold

140°C (284°F) = critical temperature for irreversible keratin structural modification (PMID 19467113). Below this: changes are reversible. Above: profound and irreversible damage to protein structure. Keep FlexStyle on medium heat. The tension of the brush does the straightening work — you don't need extreme heat.

Glycerin & Humidity

<40% RH: Glycerin helps retain moisture in hair — beneficial.
40-70% RH: Works perfectly — balanced moisture exchange.
>70% RH: Can pull excess moisture INTO hair → swells cortex → frizz.

NYM gel has glycerin (good most days). Got2b Ultra Glued has PQ-69 without glycerin (backup for extreme humidity).

"Moisture" Is Marketing

Hair with more water actually feels rougher (swollen cortex pushes cuticle scales outward). What people perceive as "moisturized" hair = smooth surface coating (cuticle lying flat). Goal: seal the cuticle with targeted silicones and ceramides, not add water.

Protein Overload Is Mostly a Myth

At normal product use levels, protein deposition plateaus — it doesn't keep building indefinitely (Croda Beauty R&D, 2025). Only intensive treatments at very high concentrations can cause stiffness. Your coarse, high-porosity hair has high capacity for protein. Use your treatments without fear.

The Dual-Lane Conflict

Curly products (PQ-69 gel) and blowout products (Polysilicone-29) use incompatible chemistry. PQ-69 residue prevents Polysilicone-29 from adhering to hair → blowout fails. Always clarify when switching from Lane 1 → Lane 2. Going Lane 2 → Lane 1 is less critical (clarifying removes the silicone seal, then gel works normally).

TE Recovery

Telogen effluvium from GLP-1/weight loss: shedding 2-4 months after trigger. Resolution: 6-12 months after weight stabilizes. Regrowth needs 12-18 months to blend with existing hair. The frizzy halo at hairline = short regrowing hairs. Time is the primary fix. Gel + directional smoothing helps cosmetically.
