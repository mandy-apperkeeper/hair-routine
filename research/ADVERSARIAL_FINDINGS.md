**Source:** [Highland Style](https://www.highland.style/blogs/learn/sulfate-free-shampoo-for-curly-hair) (2026)

**Note:** This is a brand blog (Highland sells shampoo), so it has commercial bias. However, the underlying argument is mechanistically sound.

### Analysis

**Does this challenge the EverPure assessment?** Partially.

The EverPure shampoo deep dive correctly identifies it as mild. But it doesn't address the question: *is it mild enough to adequately clean thick hair that uses multiple styling products (gel, leave-in spray, serum)?*

**Mitigating factors for Mandy's specific case:**
1. The EverPure uses FIVE mild surfactants in combination — this provides more cleaning power than a single-surfactant sulfate-free formula

- If Mandy uses NYM Curl Talk gel (PQ-69 film) + 21-in-1 (dimethicone) + Dove serum (dimethiconol) in the same routine, the EverPure shampoo may not fully remove all of that in one wash

But the deep dive should acknowledge that "mildest available" has a tradeoff: it requires periodic clarifying when heavy styling products are used. This is already implicit in the routine design but should be explicit in the education section.

**Confidence:** Medium. The concern is real but already addressed by the routine's clarifying step.

## A2: Dimethicone Buildup at Spray Concentrations

**Target report:** `research/products/loreal-21in1.md`
**Claim challenged:** "In a spray format applied to damp hair, the actual amount deposited per use is small" / "the buildup per use is minimal"

### Search Queries

1. `dimethicone leave-in spray buildup amount per application minimal negligible` — found mostly medical/pharmaceutical results, not hair-specific
2. `"silicone buildup" "leave-in" spray amount deposition hair study` — found LaTorre & Bhushan 2005 (AFM study), PMC comprehensive silicone review (2025), Scribd deposition papers
3. Attempted to access PMC12240587 (comprehensive silicone review, 2025) — **still inaccessible** (no content returned on selective search)

### Key Finding: No Direct Evidence Either Way

**What I found:**
- LaTorre & Bhushan (2005) measured silicone deposition from shampoo and conditioner using AFM — but not from leave-in sprays specifically
- The 2025 comprehensive silicone review (PMID 40641805) states: "concerns regarding product buildup from water-insoluble silicones" exist — but the full text is inaccessible (reCAPTCHA/paywall)
- CosmeticsInfo.org states silicones are "left behind as a thin coating" after rinsing — but this is about rinse-off products, not leave-ins
- No study was found that specifically measures dimethicone deposition amounts from a leave-in spray vs. a conditioner vs. a serum

**The gap:** The 21-in-1 deep dive's claim that "buildup per use is minimal" in spray format is **inferred from format logic** (spray = thin application = less product per use), not from measured data. This is a reasonable inference but it's not verified.

**What we DO know:**
- Dimethicone is water-insoluble — it does NOT wash out with sulfate-free shampoo alone
- Each application adds some amount (however small)
- Over 10-15 washes without clarifying, even small amounts accumulate
- The 21-in-1 also contains dimethiconol (position 19) — heavier, harder to remove

**Counter-argument (supporting the "it's fine" position):**
- Matrix Miracle Creator (same base formula) uses Dimethicone PEG-7 Phosphate instead of dimethicone — this is a water-soluble silicone that washes out easily. If L'Oréal's own sister brand chose to avoid dimethicone in the same product category, that suggests L'Oréal's formulators consider it a meaningful difference.

### Analysis

**The concern is neither confirmed nor dismissed.** The honest answer is:
- We don't have data on dimethicone deposition amounts from spray format specifically
- The inference that "spray = less buildup" is reasonable but unverified
- The existence of Matrix Miracle Creator (same formula minus dimethicone) suggests L'Oréal's own R&D considers the dimethicone-free version worth making
- The practical test: if Mandy notices progressive heaviness or dullness over 2-3 weeks without clarifying, the dimethicone is accumulating meaningfully

**Impact on 21-in-1 tier:** None immediately. But the Self-Critique section should be updated to note this is an unresolved question, and the Practical Education section should include a clearer "when to clarify" signal tied to this specific concern.

**Confidence:** Low. No direct evidence found either way. The question remains open.

## A5: Dove 10-in-1 Serum — Does Aminopropyl Dimethicone Outperform Amodimethicone? (RETROACTIVE)

**Date:** May 10, 2026
**Target report:** `research/products/dove-10in1-serum.md`
**Claim challenged:** "Aminopropyl dimethicone is mechanistically identical to amodimethicone in selective deposition" — is there any scenario where it's actually better?

### Search Queries

1. `"aminopropyl dimethicone" vs amodimethicone hair performance comparison` — found NaturallyCurly CurlChemist, BeautyCon, HairAide
2. Fetched BeautyCon article on bis-aminopropyl dimethicone

### Key Finding: Same Mechanism, Minor Differences in Substantivity

**From BeautyCon (NaturallyCurly):** "Bis-aminopropyl dimethicone is very similar to amodimethicone. These modified silicones have specific properties, which allow them to be excellent conditioning agents and create a protective barrier on the hair strands. They are considered the best high-performing conditioning polymers."

**From HairAide:** Bis-aminopropyl dimethicone "bonds electrostatically to damaged, negatively charged sites on the hair shaft, providing targeted conditioning where it is most needed. More substantive than standard dimethicone, meaning it stays on hair through multiple washes."

**Key distinction (from BeautyCon):** "This silicone is a pain to remove from our hair despite it not being considered a build-up silicone. Bis-aminopropyl dimethicone is a semi-water soluble silicone that may build up hair over time."

### Analysis

The Dove 10-in-1 serum uses **aminopropyl dimethicone** (mono-amine, not bis-amine). The distinction:
- **Amodimethicone** = amine-functionalized, selective, self-limiting
- **Aminopropyl dimethicone** = same amine-functionalization, same selectivity, same self-limiting behavior
- **Bis-aminopropyl dimethicone** = TWO amine groups per molecule = MORE substantive (sticks harder), potentially harder to remove

The Dove serum's aminopropyl dimethicone is functionally equivalent to amodimethicone — same mechanism, same selectivity. The "bis" version (two amine groups) is slightly more substantive but also slightly harder to remove.

**Does aminopropyl dimethicone outperform amodimethicone in any scenario?** No evidence found. They're mechanistically identical. The only difference is molecular weight/chain length variations between specific commercial grades, which aren't disclosed in INCI lists.

**Impact on Dove 10-in-1 Serum tier:** None. The original report's conclusion holds — aminopropyl dimethicone ≈ amodimethicone for practical purposes. Supporting tier confirmed.
