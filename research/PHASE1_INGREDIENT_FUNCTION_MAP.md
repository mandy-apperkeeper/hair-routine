# Phase 1: Ingredient Function Taxonomy — Mandy's Inventory

## Functional Roles in a Hair Routine

Based on research from Lab Muffin (Wong 2024), Robbins (Chemical and Physical Behavior of Human Hair, 5th ed.), INCIDecoder, NaturallyCurly (Tonya McKay), and peer-reviewed sources cited below.

---

## Role 1: Cuticle Smoothing & 18-MEA Replacement

**What it does:** Replaces the lost F-layer (18-MEA lipid layer) that cannot regenerate once damaged. Creates a smooth, hydrophobic surface that reduces friction, prevents tangling, and reflects light (shine).

**Key mechanism:** Amine-functionalized silicones (amodimethicone) carry a positive charge in acidic conditions, selectively binding to negatively-charged damaged cuticle sites. They crosslink after deposition, forming a durable film lasting multiple washes. Self-limiting — positive charges repel additional deposition, preventing buildup.

**Ingredients in Mandy's inventory:**
| Ingredient | Products | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Amodimethicone | Garnier Filler Serum, L'Oréal EverPure Bond Repair Conditioner (as bis-cetearyl amodimethicone), L'Oréal 21-in-1 Leave-In, Dove 10-in-1 Serum | Selective binding to damaged sites, crosslinks, self-limiting |
| Dimethiconol/silsesquioxane copolymer | Dove Bond Strength Conditioner, Dove Bond Strength Mask, Dove Intensive Repair Conditioner | Non-selective coating, heavier film, temporary smoothing |
| Dimethicone | L'Oréal EverPure Bond Repair Conditioner (secondary) | Non-selective uniform coating, lighter than dimethiconol |

**Coverage assessment:**
- **Targeted cuticle repair (amodimethicone):** 4 products — GOOD coverage
- **Generic smoothing (dimethiconol):** 3 products — adequate but inferior mechanism
- **Single point of failure:** None — multiple amodimethicone sources available

## Role 2: Disulfide Bond Repair (Structural)

**What it does:** Reconnects broken disulfide bonds within the hair cortex that provide structural strength and elasticity. These bonds are broken by bleaching, heat, and chemical processes.

**Key mechanism:** Bis-aminopropyl diglycol dimaleate (Olaplex's patented molecule) has maleate groups on either end that react with cysteine residues in keratin to reform disulfide bridges. This is internal structural repair, not surface conditioning.

**Ingredients in Mandy's inventory:**
| Ingredient | Products | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Bis-aminopropyl diglycol dimaleate | Olaplex 3 | True disulfide bond reconnection |
| Citric acid | Garnier Filler Pre-Shampoo, L'Oréal EverPure Bond Repair Pre-Shampoo, L'Oréal EverPure Bond Repair Shampoo/Conditioner | pH-mediated cuticle closure + non-covalent protein network reinforcement (NOT true bond repair) |

**Coverage assessment:**
- **True bond repair:** 1 product (Olaplex 3) — SINGLE POINT OF FAILURE
- **pH/ionic bond support (citric acid):** Multiple products — good coverage but different mechanism
- **Important distinction:** Citric acid does NOT reconnect disulfide bonds. Per Cosmetics & Toiletries (2021), carboxylic acids reinforce the non-covalent protein network and chelate hard water minerals, but the "bond repair" marketing from drugstore brands using citric acid is mechanistically different from Olaplex.

## Role 3: Protein Fill (Cortex Strengthening)

**What it does:** Small protein fragments penetrate the hair cortex to temporarily fill gaps left by damage, increasing strand diameter, reducing breakage, and improving elasticity.

**Key mechanism:** Hydrolyzed proteins (keratin, wheat) are broken into peptides of varying molecular weight. Low MW (<1 kDa) penetrates deep into cortex. Mid MW (1-10 kDa) penetrates outer cortex. High MW (>10 kDa) adsorbs to surface only. Per Malinauskyte et al. (Int J Cosmet Sci, 2020), both mid and high MW keratin peptides increased Young's modulus and reduced breakage.

**Ingredients in Mandy's inventory:**
| Ingredient | Products | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Triticum Vulgare (Wheat) Protein | OGX Bond Protein Repair 1-Minute Mask, OGX Bond Protein Repair Sealing Serum | Penetrates cortex, fills structural gaps |
| Hydrolyzed Keratin | OGX Bond Protein Repair Sealing Serum | Similar penetration, slightly different amino acid profile |
| SH-Polypeptide-121 | Dove Bond Strength Conditioner/Mask | Surface-level peptide, likely high MW (limited penetration) |

**Coverage assessment:**
- **Deep-penetrating protein:** 2 products (OGX 1-Minute Mask, OGX Sealing Serum) — adequate coverage
- **Surface protein:** 2 products (Dove) — supplementary only
- **No longer a single point of failure** — two OGX products provide protein fill via different delivery (rinse-off mask vs leave-on serum)

## Role 4: Humidity Barrier (Film-Forming Hold)

**What it does:** Creates a rigid or semi-rigid polymer film around hair strands that maintains integrity in high humidity, preventing water absorption that causes frizz and curl disruption.

**Key mechanism:** Cationic polymers (PQ-69 specifically) deposit via electrostatic attraction to negatively-charged hair surface, forming a continuous film. PQ-69's specific monomer composition (vinyl caprolactam + vinylpyrrolidone + DMAPMA + lauryldimonium chloride) creates a film that resists plasticization by moisture — unlike older polymers that soften and fail in humidity.

**Ingredients in Mandy's inventory:**
| Ingredient | Products | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| PQ-69 | NYM Curl Talk Flash Freeze Gel, Got2b Ultra Glued Gel | True humidity-resistant film, maintains integrity in moisture |
| PQ-37 | L'Oréal 21-in-1 Leave-In | Conditioning polymer with some hold, NOT humidity-resistant |

**Coverage assessment:**
- **True humidity barrier (PQ-69):** 2 products — RESOLVED (was critical gap, now covered)
- **Partial hold (PQ-37):** 1 product — provides light conditioning hold but not humidity resistance
- **NYM for moderate humidity, Got2b for high humidity** (Got2b has no glycerin = better in humid conditions)

## Role 5: Conditioning (Cationic Surfactant Deposition)

**What it does:** Deposits a thin layer of positively-charged surfactant molecules onto the hair cuticle, reducing friction, eliminating static, and improving wet/dry combing.

**Key mechanism:** Cationic surfactants (cetrimonium chloride, behentrimonium chloride) are attracted to the negatively-charged hair surface. They form a monolayer that smooths the cuticle, reduces inter-fiber friction, and neutralizes static charge. In lamellar systems (like Wonder Water), the surfactant is pre-organized into bilayer sheets that deposit more uniformly.

**Ingredients in Mandy's inventory:**
| Ingredient | Products | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Cetrimonium chloride | L'Oréal 8 Second Wonder Water | Lamellar deposition, rapid uniform conditioning |
| Behentrimonium chloride | L'Oréal 8 Second Wonder Water | Longer alkyl chain = more substantive deposition |
| Cetearyl alcohol + cationic surfactant (standard conditioner base) | All conditioners (Dove, L'Oréal, Garnier) | Traditional emulsion-based conditioning |

**Coverage assessment:**
- **Lamellar conditioning:** 1 product (Wonder Water) — unique delivery mechanism
- **Traditional conditioning:** Multiple products — well covered
- **No gap here**

## Role 6: Penetrating Oil (Cortex Lubrication)

**What it does:** Certain oils with specific fatty acid profiles can penetrate the hair cortex, reducing hygral fatigue (swelling/deswelling damage from water cycling) and providing internal lubrication.

**Key mechanism:** Coconut oil's lauric acid has a small molecular structure and high affinity for hair proteins, allowing it to penetrate past the cuticle into the cortex. This is unique — most oils (mineral oil, argan oil) sit on the surface only. Per Rele & Mohile (J Cosmet Sci, 2003), coconut oil reduced protein loss in both damaged and undamaged hair, while mineral oil and sunflower oil did not.

**Ingredients in Mandy's inventory:**
| Ingredient | Products | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Coconut oil | Pure Coconut Oil (standalone), OGX Miracle Oil (trace), L'Oréal 21-in-1 Leave-In (trace) | Penetrates cortex (lauric acid), reduces protein loss |
| Argan oil | OGX Moroccan Argan Oil, Garnier Filler Pre-Shampoo | Surface emollient only — does NOT penetrate cortex |

**Coverage assessment:**
- **Penetrating oil (coconut):** 1 standalone product (Pure Coconut Oil) + 2 products with trace amounts — GOOD coverage now
- **Surface emollient (argan):** 2 products — adequate
- **No gap** — pure coconut oil is the correct dedicated pre-wash tool

---

## Role 7: Humectant (Moisture Attraction)

**What it does:** Attracts water molecules from the environment and binds them to the hair shaft, increasing flexibility and reducing brittleness. Important caveat: in high humidity, humectants can attract TOO MUCH water, causing frizz.

**Key mechanism:** Panthenol (pro-vitamin B5) penetrates the hair shaft due to its small molecular size and hygroscopic structure. Once inside, it attracts and retains water, increasing strand diameter by up to 10%. It converts to pantothenic acid within the hair.

**Ingredients in Mandy's inventory:**
| Ingredient | Products | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Panthenol | OGX Bond Protein Repair 1-Minute Mask, OGX Bond Protein Repair Heat Spray | Penetrates shaft, humectant, increases diameter |
| Glycerin (likely in many formulas) | Most conditioners/treatments | Surface humectant |

**Coverage assessment:**
- **Penetrating humectant:** 2 products with panthenol (OGX Mask, OGX Heat Spray)
- **Important note for Mandy:** Humectants without a humidity barrier (Role 4) are counterproductive in high dew point conditions. They attract moisture that then disrupts the hair structure. This reinforces why the PQ-69 gel is critical.

## Role 8: Clarifying / Reset

**What it does:** Removes accumulated product buildup, mineral deposits, and excess oils to restore hair to a clean baseline. Necessary periodically to prevent diminishing returns from conditioning products.

**Key mechanism:** Surfactants (stronger than daily shampoo) dissolve and remove silicone films, polymer residue, and mineral deposits. Glycolic acid (AHA) provides chemical exfoliation of scalp and cuticle surface.

**Ingredients in Mandy's inventory:**
| Ingredient | Products | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Stronger surfactant blend | L'Oréal EverPure Clarifying Shampoo | Removes buildup without stripping |
| Glycolic acid + surfactants | L'Oréal EverPure Glossing 5-Min Lamination Mask | Chemical exfoliation + surfactant cleansing (NOT conditioning despite "mask" name) |

**Coverage assessment:**
- **Clarifying:** 2 products — adequate
- **No gap**

## Redundancy Analysis

**Over-represented functions:**
- Generic silicone smoothing (Dove products) — 3 products doing the same inferior job
- Citric acid "bond repair" — present in 4+ products but mechanism is pH adjustment, not true bond repair

**Under-represented functions:**
- True disulfide bond repair — only Olaplex

**Products that could be retired without functional loss:**
- Dove Intensive Repair Conditioner — weakest conditioner, no unique function
- Monday Moisture Leave-In — outclassed by L'Oréal 21-in-1 in every dimension (marked use-up)
