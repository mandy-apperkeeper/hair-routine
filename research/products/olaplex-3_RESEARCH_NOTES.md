## Mechanism of Action (verified, multiple sources)

### Bis-Aminopropyl Diglycol Dimaleate (BADGD)

**What it is:** An organic salt (NOT a silicone or polymer). Bifunctional Michael acceptor with maleate ions on either end and a central diglycol backbone.

**Molecular formula:** C12H20N2O8, MW 320.3 g/mol

**How it works:**
1. Targets broken sulfhydryl (-SH) groups on cysteine residues in keratin
2. The maleate groups (α,β-unsaturated carbonyl) undergo Michael addition with the exposed -SH groups
3. Because it's bifunctional (reactive groups on BOTH ends), it bridges TWO broken cysteine residues
4. This effectively reforms/creates disulfide-like crosslinks, restoring structural integrity

**Sources:**
- ResearchGate 347877744 (2020): "Structural investigation on damaged hair keratin treated with α,β-unsaturated Michael acceptors used as repairing agents" — confirms mechanism
- Dermatology Times (2025): "Bond-building treatments aim to restore hair strength by reconnecting disulfide bonds" — lists BADGD alongside hydroxypropyl gluconamide and 18-peptide technology as distinct mechanisms
- ACS C&EN (2017): "works by keeping bleaching and coloring agents from breaking disulfide bonds in the hair"
- Olaplex official: "designed to repair the millions of disulfide bonds within hair that give it structure, strength, and stability"

**Key distinction from other bond builders:**
| Product | Active | Bond Type | Mechanism |
|---------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Olaplex 3 (BADGD) | Bis-Aminopropyl Diglycol Dimaleate | Disulfide (S-S) | Michael addition — bridges broken cysteine residues |
| Garnier Pre-Shampoo | Citric Acid | Ester crosslinks | Creates NEW ester bonds between KAP hydroxyl groups |
| OGX Bond Heat Spray | Hydroxypropylgluconamide + Gluconate | Hydrogen + Ionic | Recreates H-bonds and ionic bonds in cortex |
| K18 | Bioactive peptide (K18Peptide™) | Keratin chain | Reconnects polypeptide chains (not just S-S bonds) |

**Complementarity with Garnier Pre-Shampoo:** CONFIRMED complementary. Different bond types (disulfide vs ester), different targets (keratin backbone vs KAP matrix). Using both provides multi-level structural reinforcement. This is already documented in garnier-pre-shampoo.md.

## Application Protocol

- **Format:** Pre-shampoo treatment (NOT a conditioner)
- **Application:** Damp hair, root to end, generous amount until saturated
- **Dwell time:** Minimum 10 minutes (Olaplex official). Many users leave on longer (30-60 min or overnight)
- **Frequency:** 1-2x/week for healthy hair, 2-3x/week for compromised hair
- **Rinse:** Follow with shampoo + conditioner (the treatment needs to be washed out)

## Efficacy for 2C-3A Coarse/Thick/Dry Hair

**Positive signals:**
- Disulfide bonds are the PRIMARY structural bonds in hair — coarse hair has more keratin = more disulfide bonds = more potential repair sites
- Pre-shampoo format means the treatment isn't diluted by other products
- Position 2 concentration means genuinely high dose of active
- No silicones, no heavy oils — won't weigh down curls or interfere with curl pattern
- Behentrimonium Methosulfate (pos 5) provides gentle conditioning without buildup

**Considerations for this hair profile:**
- Coarse/thick hair may need MORE product per application (longer hair, more surface area)
- The 10-minute minimum may benefit from extension to 20-30 min for thick hair (more time for BADGD to penetrate dense cortex)
- NOT a moisture treatment — provides structural repair only. Still needs conditioning step after.
- No protein in the formula (despite "bond repair" language) — no risk of protein overload from this product specifically

**Overuse concerns:**
- BADGD is NOT a protein — cannot cause protein overload
- However, over-crosslinking is theoretically possible (hair becomes too rigid/brittle)
- Reddit consensus: 1-2x/week is sufficient for most; more than 3x/week shows diminishing returns
- Some users with fine hair report stiffness with overuse — less likely with coarse hair (more bonds to repair before saturation)

## Tier Validation

**Current tier: Primary**

**Assessment:** Primary tier is JUSTIFIED. Reasons:
1. **Unique mechanism** — only product in the inventory that repairs disulfide bonds via Michael addition. No other product does this.
2. **Position 2 concentration** — highest active concentration of any bond builder at this price point
3. **Peer-reviewed evidence** — mechanism is well-established in cosmetic chemistry literature
4. **Complementary to everything else** — doesn't overlap with any other product's mechanism
5. **No adequate substitute** — Garnier pre-shampoo does different bonds (ester, not disulfide). K18 does different bonds (polypeptide chains) at 15x the price.

**The case AGAINST Primary:**
- $9-10/oz is expensive relative to other products in the routine (~$1-2/oz for most)
- The repair is described as "temporary" by some sources (bonds may re-break with subsequent chemical/heat damage)
- No independent clinical trials specifically on BADGD efficacy (Olaplex's own studies are proprietary)
- The 2023 lawsuit (dismissed) raised questions about marketing claims, though no safety issues were confirmed

**Verdict:** Primary holds. The unique mechanism with no substitute in the inventory, combined with the high active concentration, justifies the price premium. The "temporary" concern is mitigated by regular use (1-2x/week maintains the crosslinks).
