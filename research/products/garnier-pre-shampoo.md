## 1. Executive Summary

The Garnier Fructis Hair Filler Inner Fiber Repair Pre-Shampoo is a citric acid-driven crosslinking treatment disguised as a peptide product. The real active is citric acid at position 6 (high concentration) — peer-reviewed evidence (PubMed 39757966, Jan 2025) demonstrates it creates ester crosslinks between keratin-associated proteins (KAPs) in the hair cortex, increasing denaturation temperature by 6.7-15%. The "Matrixyl 3000" peptides (Palmitoyl Tripeptide-1 + Tetrapeptide-7) at positions 21-22 are almost certainly cosmetic dusting — no peer-reviewed studies exist for these peptides on hair fiber. The pre-shampoo format provides extended dwell time (5-10 min) at acidic pH, which is genuinely advantageous for citric acid crosslinking.

**Verdict:** Tier confirmed — Supporting. Complementary to Olaplex 3 (different bond types: ester crosslinks vs disulfide bonds). The established decision "pre-shampoo treatment is marketing" needs nuance — citric acid crosslinking is a legitimate, peer-reviewed mechanism that benefits from pre-shampoo dwell time.

## 3. Mechanism Assessment

### 3A. Citric Acid (Position 6) — PRIMARY ACTIVE: Ester Crosslinking

**Verified (peer-reviewed):** "CA treatment demonstrably enhances the structural integrity of chemically treated hair. DSC revealed increased crosslinking density of keratin-associated proteins (KAPs) in the hair cortex, evidenced by a 6.7% to 15.0% increase in denaturation temperature." [PubMed 39757966, Jan 2025]

**Mechanism:** Citric acid (a tricarboxylic acid) forms ester crosslinks between hydroxyl groups on keratin-associated proteins (KAPs) in the hair cortex. These are NEW covalent bonds — not reforming broken ones (as Olaplex does with disulfide bonds), but creating additional structural connections that reinforce the protein matrix.

**Position significance:** Position 6 of 23 = HIGH concentration. This is not a pH adjuster at trace levels — it's a functional active, likely in the 1-5% range. The sodium citrate at position 8 acts as a buffer to maintain the acidic pH needed for crosslinking without over-acidifying (which would damage the hair).

**Pre-shampoo context:** The acidic pH environment + extended dwell time (5-10 minutes on wet hair) is genuinely advantageous for this mechanism:
1. Wet hair swells, opening the cuticle and allowing citric acid access to the cortex
2. Acidic pH promotes ester bond formation between citric acid's carboxyl groups and KAP hydroxyl groups
3. Extended contact time allows more crosslinks to form before rinsing
4. The subsequent shampoo step removes excess citric acid while the crosslinks (covalent bonds) remain

**Comparison to Olaplex 3:**
| Aspect | Garnier Pre-Shampoo (Citric Acid) | Olaplex 3 (Bis-aminopropyl diglycol dimaleate) |
|--------|-----------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Bond type | NEW ester crosslinks between KAPs | REFORMS broken disulfide bonds (S-S) |
| Target | Keratin-associated proteins (matrix) | Keratin chains (structural backbone) |
| Mechanism | Creates additional structural connections | Reconnects severed structural bonds |
| Evidence level | Peer-reviewed (PubMed 39757966, Jan 2025) | Peer-reviewed (multiple studies) |
| Complementary? | **YES** — different bond types, different targets | **YES** — different bond types, different targets |

**Assessment:** Citric acid at this concentration and contact time is a legitimate cortex-level treatment. The peer-reviewed evidence is strong (DSC measurement of crosslinking density is a rigorous methodology). This is NOT marketing — it's a real mechanism with quantified outcomes.

### 3B. Palmitoyl Tripeptide-1 + Palmitoyl Tetrapeptide-7 (Positions 21-22) — "Matrixyl 3000"

**Verified (for skin):** These two peptides together = Matrixyl 3000. Palmitoyl Tripeptide-1 (GHK fragment) stimulates collagen synthesis (types I, III, IV, VII) and fibronectin. Palmitoyl Tetrapeptide-7 (GQPR) is a signaling peptide involved in dermal homeostasis. [Typology UK, 2025, citing TIAN J. et al. 2020, WANG Y. et al. 2024]

**For hair (Unknown):** NO peer-reviewed studies were found demonstrating Matrixyl 3000's efficacy on hair fiber. The peptides are lipophilic (palmitoylated) which could theoretically aid penetration into the hair shaft, but this is unverified for hair specifically. Garnier's "bond repair" claim for the peptides is extrapolated from skin science, not validated on hair.

**Position significance:** Positions 21-22 of 23 = VERY low concentration. Likely 0.001-0.01% range. At these concentrations, even if the peptides had a hair-specific mechanism, the dose is almost certainly sub-functional.

**Assessment:** Cosmetic dusting for marketing purposes. The "15% concentrate of peptides, citric acid, and bond repair complex" claim likely means 15% of a proprietary blend that CONTAINS these ingredients — not 15% peptides. The actual peptide concentration is almost certainly below 0.1%. The citric acid is doing the real work.

### 3C. Behentrimonium Chloride (Position 4) — Conditioning/Protection

Same C22 cationic quat as in the daily conditioner. In the pre-shampoo context, it serves a dual purpose:
1. **Protective:** Coats the hair before the subsequent shampoo step, reducing protein loss from surfactant exposure
2. **Distribution:** Helps the treatment spread evenly through thick hair

### 3D. Glycerin (Position 3) — Humectant

High position = high concentration. Draws moisture into the hair shaft. In a pre-shampoo context, helps hydrate the cortex before the drying effects of shampoo. The hydrated, swollen cortex may also be more accessible to citric acid crosslinking.

### 3E. Polyquaternium-10 (Position 12) — Film-Forming Polymer

Deposits a thin cationic film on hair. Provides slip and reduces static. May help the citric acid crosslinks survive the subsequent shampoo step by creating a protective layer over the treated hair.

### 3F. Sodium Citrate (Position 8) — pH Buffer

Works with citric acid to maintain the target acidic pH. Without the buffer, the high citric acid concentration could over-acidify and damage the hair. The citrate/citric acid buffer system maintains a stable pH in the optimal range for crosslinking without going too low.

### 3G. Arginine (Position 19) — Amino Acid

Same as in the Garnier conditioner. Provides protein support and pH buffering. At position 19, low concentration — supporting role only.

### 3H. Argania Spinosa Kernel Oil (Position 18) — Argan Oil

Surface emollient. Provides slip and shine. Same role as in the conditioner.

## 8. Practical Education Section (for in-app use)

### Why you're using this

This is your structural reinforcement step — it creates new connections inside your hair fiber that weren't there before. The citric acid (the real active, despite the peptide marketing) forms ester crosslinks between proteins in your hair's cortex. Think of it like adding extra rivets to a bridge — the bridge was functional before, but now it's stronger. This is a different kind of repair than Olaplex (which reconnects broken bonds) — this one adds NEW connections.

### How to know it's working

- Hair feels stronger and more resilient over time (not immediate — crosslinks accumulate with repeated use)
- Less breakage during detangling and styling (stronger cortex = more mechanical resistance)
- Hair holds up better to heat styling (higher denaturation temperature = more heat tolerance)
- Reduced split ends over weeks of use (stronger fiber = less splitting under stress)
- The benefit is cumulative — you won't feel a dramatic difference after one use, but after 4-6 uses the structural improvement becomes noticeable

### How to know it's NOT working

- No reduction in breakage after 6+ uses → your hair may not be damaged enough to benefit significantly (intact KAPs don't need additional crosslinks). This is good news.
- Hair feels dry or brittle after use → you may be leaving it on too long or not conditioning adequately afterward. The acidic pH can temporarily tighten the cuticle — follow with your regular conditioner.
- Scalp irritation → citric acid at this concentration can irritate sensitive scalps. Apply to lengths and ends only, avoiding the scalp.

### Why this over alternatives

- **vs. Olaplex 3 alone:** They're complementary, not competing. Olaplex fixes broken disulfide bonds (structural backbone). This creates new ester crosslinks (protein matrix). Using both = maximum structural reinforcement from two different angles.
- **vs. just using the EverPure Bond Shampoo:** The shampoo has citric acid at lower concentration with ~2 min contact time. This pre-shampoo delivers significantly more crosslinking per use (higher concentration × longer dwell time = more bonds formed).
- **vs. skipping it entirely:** The routine works without it. But if your hair is chemically treated, heat-damaged, or in TE recovery, the additional structural support is measurably beneficial (6.7-15% increase in thermal stability per the peer-reviewed study).

### When to reach for something else

- **Acute bond breakage** (post-bleach, post-relaxer) → Olaplex 3 first (reforms the broken disulfide bonds), then this on the next wash (adds ester crosslinks to the weakened matrix)
- **Protein overload symptoms** (stiff, brittle) → Skip this — you don't need more structural reinforcement. Focus on moisture.
- **Healthy, undamaged hair** → This provides diminishing returns on hair that already has intact protein structure. Use less frequently (every 2-3 washes instead of every wash).

### If you're shopping for a replacement

Key things to look for:
- **Citric acid in the top 10 ingredients** (not just as a pH adjuster at the end of the list)
- **Pre-shampoo or treatment format** with 5+ minute dwell time
- **Acidic pH** (check for citric acid + sodium citrate buffer system)
- **No sulfates** in the treatment itself (would strip the crosslinks during application)

What makes this one good value:
- $0.89-0.99/oz for a treatment with peer-reviewed structural benefit
- 10.1 oz bottle lasts months at 1-2x/week use
- The citric acid mechanism is the same regardless of brand — you're paying for concentration and format, not a proprietary molecule
