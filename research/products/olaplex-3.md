## 1. Executive Summary

Olaplex No. 3 is a pre-shampoo treatment built around a single patented active ingredient: Bis-Aminopropyl Diglycol Dimaleate (BADGD) at position 2 — immediately after water, indicating an unusually high concentration for a bond-building active. BADGD is a bifunctional Michael acceptor that bridges broken cysteine residues in keratin, effectively reforming disulfide bonds — the permanent structural crosslinks that give hair its strength, elasticity, and shape. This is a fundamentally different mechanism from every other bond repair product in the inventory: the OGX Bond line repairs hydrogen and ionic bonds (weaker, more numerous), Garnier Pre-Shampoo creates new ester crosslinks in the KAP matrix, and K18 reconnects polypeptide chains. Only Olaplex targets the disulfide bonds that form the structural backbone of keratin.

**Verdict:** Primary tier is correct and strongly justified. This is the only product in the inventory — and one of very few on the market at any price — that repairs disulfide bonds via Michael addition chemistry. The mechanism is peer-reviewed, the concentration is high (position 2), and there is no substitute at this price point. The product is complementary to (not competing with) every other bond repair product in the inventory because it targets a different bond type. For post-TE recovery hair with accumulated structural damage, this is the heavy-duty repair tool that nothing else replaces.

**Notable:** BADGD at position 2 is extraordinary. In most "bond repair" products, the active is buried at positions 7-12 (0.2-1% concentration). Position 2 suggests 3-8% BADGD — a genuinely therapeutic dose, not a marketing trace. The rest of the formula is a simple conditioning cream base (cetearyl alcohol + behentrimonium methosulfate + cetyl alcohol) designed to deliver the active and provide enough slip for even distribution. No silicones. No heavy oils. No protein. The formula is deliberately minimal — it's a delivery vehicle for BADGD, not a multi-benefit conditioner.
