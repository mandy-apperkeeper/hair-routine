## 1. Executive Summary

The Monday Moisture Leave-In Conditioner is a basic dimethicone-first leave-in with jojoba oil, almond oil, and Polyquaternium-37 as its functional ingredients. Dimethicone (position 2) provides non-selective silicone coating — it deposits uniformly on all hair regardless of damage level, which means it builds up on healthy sections while providing less targeted repair than amodimethicone-based products. PQ-37 provides light hold and film-forming. The formula lacks any selective conditioning, UV protection, or bond repair mechanisms.

**Verdict:** Use-Up tier confirmed. Outclassed by L'Oréal 21-in-1 on every axis relevant to this hair profile: the 21-in-1 has amodimethicone (selective), coconut oil (penetrating), UV filters, and more substantive conditioning. The Monday product is adequate but generic — finish the bottle, don't repurchase.

## 3. Mechanism Assessment

### Mechanism 1: Dimethicone Non-Selective Coating (Position 2)

**Verified:** Dimethicone "coats hair shafts, locks in moisture, and enhances shine, though it can cause buildup, especially on fine or oily hair." It "creates smoothness, reduces friction and improves shine" but deposits uniformly rather than selectively. ([Wimpole Clinic, 2026](https://wimpoleclinic.com/blog/dimethicone-for-hair/); [An'du, 2025](https://www.an-du.co.uk/blogs/hair-care/why-your-conditioner-might-be-causing-build-up))

**Verified:** Unlike amodimethicone which "selectively binds to damaged, negatively charged areas of the hair cuticle rather than coating uniformly," dimethicone deposits everywhere equally. This means it builds up on healthy sections unnecessarily while providing the same amount to damaged sections that need more. ([HairAide, 2025](https://hairaide.com/ingredients/amodimethicone/))

**Verified:** Per CosmeticsInfo.org: "Following water rinsing of a shampoo or conditioner, the silicone is left behind as a thin coating over each individual hair shaft to fill in visible defects in the hair cuticle, improving combing ease." ([CosmeticsInfo.org, 2025](https://www.cosmeticsinfo.org/ingredient/dimethicone/))

**Analysis:** Dimethicone at position 2 (highest concentration active) means this product is primarily a silicone coating delivery vehicle. It provides smoothing, shine, and frizz reduction — but non-selectively. For 2C-3A hair with variable damage along the strand (TE regrowth = different damage levels at different points), selective conditioning (amodimethicone) is categorically superior. Dimethicone also requires sulfate or clarifying shampoo to remove completely, contributing to buildup cycles.

### Mechanism 2: Jojoba Oil (Position 4) + Almond Oil (Position 8)

**Verified:** Jojoba oil is technically a liquid wax ester, not a triglyceride oil. It does NOT penetrate the hair cortex (unlike coconut oil's lauric acid). It sits on the surface as an emollient film, providing slip and shine. ([Established in prior research — coconut oil penetrates due to lauric acid's keratin affinity; jojoba and argan do not penetrate beyond the outer 5µm](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-9284/11/2/64))

**Inferred:** Sweet almond oil is a triglyceride oil rich in oleic acid (not lauric acid). Like jojoba, it does not significantly penetrate the hair cortex. It provides surface emolliency and some cuticle smoothing but no internal structural benefit.

**Analysis:** Both oils are surface-only emollients. They provide slip, reduce friction during combing, and add shine — but they don't penetrate or repair. For coarse, dry hair that needs internal moisture retention, penetrating oils (coconut) or selective silicones (amodimethicone) are more effective.

### Mechanism 3: Polyquaternium-37 (Position 9)

**Verified:** PQ-37 is a cationic conditioning polymer that provides light hold and film-forming properties. It's commonly used in leave-in products for its ability to deposit on hair and provide some anti-static and conditioning benefits without stiffness.

**Analysis:** PQ-37 is a lighter conditioning polymer than PQ-7 (used in the EverPure shampoo). It provides adequate conditioning for a leave-in but is not particularly substantive. The L'Oréal 21-in-1 achieves its conditioning through amodimethicone (selective, self-limiting) which is a fundamentally better mechanism for damaged hair.

### Mechanism 4: Glycerin (Position 5)

**Verified:** Glycerin is a humectant that draws moisture from the environment to the hair surface. In moderate humidity (dew point 35-60°F), this is beneficial. In very high humidity, it can draw excess moisture into the cortex (contributing to frizz). In very low humidity, it can draw moisture OUT of hair into the dry air.

**Analysis:** Glycerin at position 5 is a meaningful concentration. For this hair profile in variable humidity conditions, glycerin is a double-edged sword — helpful on moderate days, potentially counterproductive on extreme days. The app's dew point detection already accounts for humidity-dependent product behavior.

## 4. Comparison to Primary Leave-In (L'Oréal 21-in-1)

| Feature | Monday Moisture Leave-In | L'Oréal 21-in-1 |
|---------|--------------------------|------------------|
| Silicone type | Dimethicone (non-selective, builds up) | Amodimethicone (selective, self-limiting) |
| Penetrating oil | None (jojoba + almond = surface only) | Coconut oil (lauric acid penetrates cortex) |
| UV protection | None | Ethylhexyl Methoxycinnamate (UV filter) |
| Conditioning polymer | PQ-37 (light) | Amodimethicone IS the polymer |
| Bond repair | None | None |
| Buildup risk | Higher (dimethicone accumulates) | Lower (amodimethicone self-limits) |
| Humidity response | Glycerin (variable — can backfire) | Coconut oil (stable internal protection) |

**Verdict:** The L'Oréal 21-in-1 is superior on every dimension that matters for this hair profile:
1. Selective vs non-selective silicone (targeted repair vs uniform coating)
2. Penetrating oil vs surface-only oils (internal protection vs external slip)
3. UV protection vs none (photoprotection for outdoor exposure)
4. Lower buildup risk (self-limiting vs accumulating)

## 5. Tier Placement Validation

**Current tier:** Use-Up

**Assessment:** Correct. This product is adequate but outclassed.

**What it does well:**
- Provides basic smoothing and frizz reduction (dimethicone)
- Adds surface slip for detangling (jojoba + almond oils)
- Light hold without stiffness (PQ-37)
- Affordable and widely available

**Why it's Use-Up, not Supporting or Primary:**
- Non-selective silicone is the wrong tool for variable-damage hair
- No penetrating oils means no internal protection
- No UV protection
- Contributes to buildup that requires clarifying to remove
- Every function it performs, the L'Oréal 21-in-1 does better with fewer downsides

**What would change this:** Nothing in the current formulation. A reformulation adding amodimethicone and removing dimethicone would make it competitive, but that would make it a different product.

**Verdict:** Finish the bottle. Do not repurchase. The L'Oréal 21-in-1 is the correct primary leave-in for this hair profile.

## 6. Practical Education Section

### Why you're using this up

You have this bottle and it's not harmful — it provides basic conditioning and smoothing. But every wash day you use it instead of the 21-in-1, you're getting non-selective coating instead of targeted repair, surface oils instead of penetrating protection, and no UV defense. It's like using a butter knife when you have a scalpel available.

### How to use it while finishing

- Apply sparingly to mid-lengths and ends only (avoid roots — dimethicone buildup near scalp looks greasy)
- Use on days when you're staying indoors (no UV protection)
- Can layer under the 21-in-1 if you want extra slip for detangling, but this is redundant
- Monitor for buildup signs (hair feeling coated, heavy, or dull) — clarify if needed

### When it's gone

Replace with nothing — the L'Oréal 21-in-1 covers the leave-in step completely. No need for a second leave-in product.
