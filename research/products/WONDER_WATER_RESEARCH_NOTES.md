## Mechanism: Lamellar Technology

**Key insight from Allure (cosmetic chemist Ni'Kita Wilson):**
- "Lamellars are anything comprised of thin, flat layers of alternating materials"
- The positively-charged materials are "highly unstable and ready to cling to whatever they can — like the negatively-charged spots where your hair is damaged"
- Result: shinier, softer, silkier without being weighed down

**From UL Prospector (lamellar gel network primer):**
- Lamellar gel networks = combinations of low-HLB + high-HLB crystalline surfactants forming colloidal bilayer structures
- In Wonder Water: Myristyl Alcohol (low-HLB, fatty alcohol) + Cetrimonium/Behentrimonium Chloride (high-HLB, cationic surfactants)
- The bilayer sheets deposit rapidly on contact — explains the "8 second" claim
- Pre-organized into bilayer sheets = more uniform deposition than traditional emulsion conditioners

**From existing project research (PHASE1, PHASE2, PHASE3):**
- Lamellar delivery is "uniquely efficient — rapid deposition despite short contact"
- Enhances subsequent product deposition (smoother cuticle = better leave-in distribution)
- "Efficiency booster, not a unique function provider" — conditioner already provides cationic conditioning
- Wonder Water makes it faster and more uniform

## Product variants (important context)

L'Oréal has multiple Wonder Water versions:
1. **Original "8 Second Wonder Water"** — the one in Mandy's inventory (INCI above)
2. **Hyaluron Plump Wonder Water** — newer version with sodium acetylated hyaluronate added
3. **Dream Lengths Wonder Water** — EU/international version with slightly different INCI order
4. **Colour Protect Wonder Water** — has coconut oil + red dye added

All share the same core platform (propylene glycol + alcohol denat + myristyl alcohol + cetrimonium/behentrimonium chloride). The original is the simplest and cleanest.
