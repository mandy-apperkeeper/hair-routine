## Key Mechanisms Found

### 1. Octylacrylamide/Acrylates/Butylaminoethyl Methacrylate Copolymer (Position 3) — PRIMARY ACTIVE: Film-Forming Hold Polymer
- **Verified:** Trade name: Amphomer. "An exceptionally hard holding acrylic polymer that is widely used in a variety of hair styling aids. This amphoteric acrylic polymer has outstanding curl retention properties. It is a stiff resin with unparalleled holding power for ultimate control in all temperature and humidity conditions." [Essential Ingredients / Nouryon, UL Prospector]
- **Verified:** "A precision film-forming polymer designed to create a durable, continuous film on the surface. This advanced property significantly enhances water resistance, longevity, and overall adherence." [Moumoujus]
- **Verified:** Functions as "antistatic agent, film former, hair fixative" — "enables the shaping of hairstyles." [Altmeyers.org]
- **Mechanism:** When the alcohol/propellant evaporates, this polymer deposits as a rigid film on the hair surface. The film holds strands in place with extreme stiffness. The "amphoteric" nature (both positive and negative charges) means it adheres well to hair regardless of damage state.
- **Position significance:** Position 3 of 10 = HIGH concentration. After the solvents (alcohol denat + HFC-152a), this is the primary functional ingredient. Estimated 5-15% of the formula.
- **Humidity resistance:** The polymer film is water-resistant, which is why the hold survives humidity. This is the same polymer class used in professional-grade hairsprays.

### 2. Alcohol Denat. (Position 1) — Solvent/Carrier
- **Role:** Primary solvent that dissolves the hold polymer and evaporates rapidly after spraying. The fast evaporation is what creates the "instant freeze" effect — the polymer deposits immediately as the alcohol flashes off.
- **Concern for dry hair:** Very high alcohol content (position 1 = likely 40-60% of formula). Repeated use on already-dry hair can exacerbate dryness. However, as a finishing spray applied to styled hair (not to wet/damp hair), the alcohol evaporates before it can significantly dehydrate the hair shaft.

### 3. Hydrofluorocarbon 152a (Position 2) — Aerosol Propellant
- **Role:** Creates the spray mechanism. Evaporates instantly upon leaving the can. No functional role on hair.
- **Environmental note:** HFC-152a has a global warming potential (GWP) of 124 — lower than older propellants but still a greenhouse gas. The UK formulation uses dimethyl ether (GWP ~1) instead.

### 4. Aminomethyl Propanol (Position 4) — pH Adjuster/Neutralizer
- **Role:** Neutralizes the acidic polymer (Amphomer) to make it soluble in the alcohol base. Also adjusts pH for optimal film formation. Standard helper ingredient in hairspray formulations.

### 5. Triethyl Citrate (Position 6) — Plasticizer
- **Role:** Softens the polymer film slightly to prevent it from being TOO brittle. Without a plasticizer, the Amphomer film would crack and flake. Triethyl citrate provides just enough flexibility for the film to move with the hair without breaking apart, while maintaining the "freeze" hold.
- **Key insight:** This is what makes the Got2b "blasting freeze" different from a truly rigid spray — it has enough plasticizer to prevent flaking but not so much that it loses hold.

### 6. PEG/PPG-17/18 Dimethicone + PPG-12 Dimethicone (Positions 7, 10) — Silicone Conditioning
- **Role:** Provide a small amount of conditioning/shine to the finished style. Prevent the polymer film from looking completely matte/dry. At positions 7 and 10 of 10, these are LOW concentration — just enough to add a touch of shine without compromising hold.

### 7. AMP-Isostearoyl Hydrolyzed Wheat Protein (Position 8) — Conditioning Protein
- **Verified:** "An aminomethyl propanol salt of the condensation product of isostearoyl chloride with Hydrolyzed Wheat Protein." Functions as hair conditioning agent. [EWG, CosIng]
- **Role:** A lipophilic (fat-loving) protein derivative that provides conditioning within the hold film. Helps prevent the spray from making hair feel completely stripped/dry. At position 8 of 10, this is a trace inclusion — enough to slightly soften the feel without reducing hold.
- **Interesting:** This is a protein-lipid hybrid — the wheat protein is conjugated with isostearic acid, making it oil-soluble and able to integrate into the polymer film rather than sitting on top of it.

## Relevance for 2C-3A Coarse/Thick/Dry Hair

### Use Case in Curly Hair Routine
- **NOT a curl-defining product.** This is a finishing/hold spray, not a styling gel. It doesn't define curls — it locks in whatever style is already set.
- **Potential uses:**
  1. Lock in a blowout after using the Diamond Sleek/Marc Anthony (prevent reversion)
  2. Hold an updo or protective style in place
  3. Freeze flyaways/baby hairs along the hairline
  4. Set pin curls or roller sets
  5. Final layer over gel cast for extra humidity resistance on high-humidity days

### Comparison to NYM Curl Talk Gel (the primary curl styler in inventory)
| Aspect | Got2b Freeze Spray | NYM Curl Talk Gel |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Purpose** | Lock in finished style | Define and hold curls during drying |
| **Application** | Dry hair, after styling | Wet/damp hair, before drying |
| **Hold type** | Rigid, non-flexible | Flexible, scrunchable cast |
| **Mechanism** | Amphomer rigid film | PVP/VA + polyquaternium flexible film |
| **Curl definition** | None (preserves existing) | Yes (encourages clumping) |
| **Scrunch-out-the-crunch** | No — stays rigid | Yes — softens when scrunched |
| **Best for** | Blowouts, updos, flyaways | Wash-and-go curl days |

**Key insight:** These are NOT competing products. They serve completely different roles in the routine. The gel defines curls on wash day; the freeze spray locks in blowouts or updos on styling days.

## Contradictions

1. **"Washes out easily" vs "lasts until your next shampoo":** Both claims are from Schwarzkopf. The Amphomer polymer IS water-soluble (it dissolves in water when shampooed), so both claims are technically true — it holds until you wash, then washes out cleanly. No buildup concern.
2. **"Humidity-resistant" vs high alcohol content:** The alcohol evaporates immediately (it's the carrier, not a leave-on ingredient). The humidity resistance comes from the Amphomer film, not the alcohol. So the product IS humidity-resistant despite being alcohol-based.

## Key Findings for Write Session

1. **This is a focused, single-purpose product** — 10 ingredients, one job: extreme hold via Amphomer rigid film. No pretense of repair, moisture, or treatment.
2. **Supporting tier is correct.** It's a finishing spray for specific styling days, not a daily essential for curly hair routines.
3. **Complementary to (not competing with) the NYM Curl Talk Gel.** Gel = curl definition on wash day. Freeze spray = lock in blowouts/updos on styling days. Different tools for different days.
4. **Excellent value** at $0.54-0.66/fl oz for a 12 oz can. One of the cheapest products in the inventory.
5. **The Amphomer polymer is the gold standard** for rigid hold in hairsprays. Well-established technology with decades of use.
6. **Alcohol content is high but not a concern** for this use case — it evaporates instantly as a carrier, not a leave-on ingredient. The finished film contains no alcohol.
7. **For the app:** This product's step category is "Finishing/Hold" (applied to dry, styled hair). Its intelligence metadata should note: use on blowout days, updo days, or high-humidity days as a final seal. NOT for curl definition days (use gel instead).
8. **Skip adversarial** — supporting tier, uncontested placement, no claims that would change app behavior if wrong.
