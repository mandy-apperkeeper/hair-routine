## Key Mechanisms Found

### 1. Isododecane (Position 1) — Volatile Carrier
- **Verified:** Isododecane is a volatile hydrocarbon emollient/solvent. Evaporates after application, leaving behind the non-volatile ingredients. [INCIDecoder]
- **Significance:** This is a leave-in serum with a lightweight, fast-evaporating base — similar concept to the Dove 10-in-1's cyclopentasiloxane carrier. After evaporation, the dimethicone/dimethiconol deposit remains on hair.

### 2. Dimethicone (Position 4) + Dimethiconol (Position 6) — Silicone Coating
- **Verified:** Dimethicone is a non-volatile silicone polymer that coats hair, providing smoothing, shine, and frizz control. It forms a protective barrier. [INCIDecoder, Wimpole Clinic 2025]
- **Verified:** Dimethiconol is a higher molecular weight silicone that provides "intense smoothing, detangling, and shine. Heavier than dimethicone, it provides longer-lasting frizz control and moisture sealing." [HairAide, 2025]
- **Buildup concern:** Both are non-volatile silicones that accumulate with repeated use. For 2C-3A curly/wavy hair, this can weigh down curls and require clarifying shampoo to remove. [Van + Veronica, 2024; Wimpole Clinic, 2025]
- **Position significance:** Positions 4 and 6 = moderate-to-high concentration. These are the primary functional ingredients after the carrier evaporates.

### 3. Ascorbyl Glucoside / "Vitamin Cg" (Position 15) — MARKETING INGREDIENT
- **Verified:** Ascorbyl glucoside is a stable vitamin C derivative. Its mechanism: enzymatic hydrolysis releases bioactive vitamin C that provides antioxidant protection. [ChemicalBook, 2025; INCIDecoder]
- **Hair-specific evidence:** NONE FOUND. All peer-reviewed studies on ascorbyl glucoside are for SKIN (antioxidant, collagen synthesis, melanin inhibition). No published research demonstrates ascorbyl glucoside repairing hair fiber structure.
- **Position significance:** Position 15 of 27 = LOW concentration (likely 0.1-0.5%). Below the preservative (phenoxyethanol at pos 10, max 1%). This is a marketing-level inclusion.
- **Theoretical hair benefit (Inferred):** Antioxidant protection against UV/environmental oxidative damage to hair proteins. But at this concentration and in a leave-in format competing with silicone coating, the functional benefit is questionable.
- **Garnier's claim:** "Vitamin Cg strengthens broken bonds within the hair cortex, yielding up to 79% more strength." [Garnier.ca] — This claim is for the SYSTEM (pre-shampoo + shampoo + conditioner + serum together), not the serum alone. The strength benefit likely comes from the pre-shampoo's citric acid crosslinking, not the serum's vitamin C.

### 4. Citric Acid (Position 21) — pH ADJUSTER ONLY
- **Critical distinction from pre-shampoo:** In the pre-shampoo, citric acid is at position 6 (high concentration, functional active for KAP crosslinking). In this serum, it's at position 21 of 27 = TRACE amount, functioning only as a pH adjuster/buffer.
- **No crosslinking function at this concentration.** The peer-reviewed evidence for citric acid crosslinking (PubMed 39757966) used concentrated solutions with extended dwell time. Position 21 in a leave-in serum is orders of magnitude below functional concentration.

### 5. Hydroxyethyl Urea (Position 9) — Humectant
- **Verified:** A urea derivative that works as a strong moisturizer/humectant. Similar moisturizing ability to glycerin but with a non-sticky, non-tacky feel. [INCIDecoder]
- **Position significance:** Position 9 = moderate concentration. Provides some hydration benefit beneath the silicone layer.

### 6. PEG-14 Dimethicone (Position 8) — Emulsifier/Silicone Surfactant
- Helps the water-phase ingredients (glycerin, hydroxyethyl urea) mix with the silicone-phase ingredients. Functional helper, not an active.

### 7. Alcohol Denat (Position 5) — Solvent/Penetration Enhancer
- **Concern:** At position 5, this is a significant amount of denatured alcohol. It aids in spreading and quick-drying but can be drying to hair with repeated use. The silicone coating may mitigate this, but it's worth noting for already-dry hair types.

### 8. Arginine (Position 20) — Amino Acid (trace)
- Same as in the pre-shampoo and conditioner. At position 20, this is a trace inclusion — likely for marketing ("repair care complex") rather than functional benefit.

## What This Product Actually Is (Honest Assessment)

**This is a silicone smoothing serum with a vitamin C marketing angle.**

The functional mechanism is straightforward:
1. Isododecane carrier spreads the product evenly through hair
2. Carrier evaporates
3. Dimethicone + dimethiconol deposit remains, coating the hair shaft
4. Coating provides: smoothing, frizz control, shine, heat protection, reduced friction between strands

The "Vitamin Cg" (ascorbyl glucoside) at position 15 is a marketing ingredient at sub-functional concentration for hair repair. The "citric acid" at position 21 is a pH adjuster, not a crosslinking agent. The "repair care complex" (arginine + citric acid + ascorbyl glucoside) is marketing nomenclature for trace-level ingredients.

**The "79% stronger" claim** is for the full 4-step system. The pre-shampoo (citric acid at position 6, 5-10 min dwell time) is doing the actual structural repair. The serum's contribution is surface coating/protection, not internal fiber repair.

## Relevance for 2C-3A Coarse/Thick/Dry Hair

**Pros:**
- Smoothing and frizz control (the silicones work regardless of marketing claims)
- Lightweight carrier means it won't feel heavy initially
- Hydroxyethyl urea provides some humectant benefit

**Cons:**
- Non-volatile silicone buildup is a real concern for curly/wavy hair — can weigh down curl pattern over time
- Requires clarifying shampoo to fully remove (the EverPure clarifying shampoo in the inventory handles this)
- No selective deposition — coats healthy and damaged areas equally
- Alcohol denat at position 5 may contribute to dryness between washes
- More expensive per oz than alternatives that offer selective silicone technology

**Bottom line:** This product works as a basic silicone serum, but it's outclassed by the Dove 10-in-1 (selective silicone, cheaper) and the L'Oréal 21-in-1 (selective silicone + penetrating oil + UV filter, cheaper). The "Vitamin Cg" differentiation is marketing, not mechanism.

## Contradictions

1. **"Fills hair with strength 7 layers deep"** — The serum's silicones coat the OUTSIDE of the hair shaft. They do not penetrate 7 layers into the cortex. This claim may be valid for the pre-shampoo (citric acid crosslinking) but not for a silicone leave-in serum.
2. **"Inner fiber repair"** — Dimethicone and dimethiconol are surface-coating agents. They do not repair internal fiber structure. The "inner fiber repair" claim is for the system, not the serum alone.
3. **"Vitamin Cg strengthens broken bonds"** — No peer-reviewed evidence supports ascorbyl glucoside strengthening hair bonds. The bond-strengthening in the system comes from citric acid crosslinking in the pre-shampoo.
