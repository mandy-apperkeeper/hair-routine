## 1. Executive Summary

The Got2b Glued Blasting Freeze Spray is a focused, single-purpose finishing spray with only 10 ingredients. The primary active is Octylacrylamide/Acrylates/Butylaminoethyl Methacrylate Copolymer (trade name: Amphomer) — a rigid film-forming polymer that deposits as a stiff, humidity-resistant coating when the alcohol carrier evaporates. This is NOT a curl definer — it's a finishing spray that locks in whatever style is already set (blowouts, updos, pin curls, flyaway control). It's complementary to the NYM Curl Talk Gel, not competing with it: gel defines curls on wash day, freeze spray locks in blowouts on styling days.

**Verdict:** Supporting tier is correct. This is a finishing tool for specific styling days (blowouts, updos, events), not a daily essential for a curly hair routine. Best-in-class for its specific function at excellent value ($0.54-0.66/fl oz for a 12 oz can).

## 3. Mechanism Assessment

### 3A. Octylacrylamide/Acrylates/Butylaminoethyl Methacrylate Copolymer (Position 3) — PRIMARY ACTIVE: Amphomer Rigid Film Polymer

**Verified:** Trade name: Amphomer. "An exceptionally hard holding acrylic polymer that is widely used in a variety of hair styling aids. This amphoteric acrylic polymer has outstanding curl retention properties. It is a stiff resin with unparalleled holding power for ultimate control in all temperature and humidity conditions." [Essential Ingredients / Nouryon, UL Prospector]

**Verified:** "A precision film-forming polymer designed to create a durable, continuous film on the surface. This advanced property significantly enhances water resistance, longevity, and overall adherence." [Moumoujus]

**Verified:** Functions as "antistatic agent, film former, hair fixative" — "enables the shaping of hairstyles." [Altmeyers.org]

**Mechanism:** When the alcohol/propellant evaporates (instantly upon spraying), this polymer deposits as a rigid film on the hair surface. The film holds strands in place with extreme stiffness. The "amphoteric" nature (both positive and negative charges) means it adheres well to hair regardless of damage state — it grips healthy and damaged hair equally.

**Position significance:** Position 3 of 10 = HIGH concentration. After the solvents (alcohol denat + HFC-152a propellant), this is the primary functional ingredient. Estimated 5-15% of the formula.

**Humidity resistance:** The polymer film is water-resistant, which is why the hold survives humidity. This is the same polymer class used in professional-grade hairsprays. The film doesn't dissolve in atmospheric moisture — only in liquid water (shampooing).

### 3B. Alcohol Denat. (Position 1) — Volatile Solvent/Carrier

**Role:** Primary solvent that dissolves the hold polymer and evaporates rapidly after spraying. The fast evaporation creates the "instant freeze" effect — the polymer deposits immediately as the alcohol flashes off.

**Concentration:** Position 1 = likely 40-60% of formula. Very high alcohol content.

**Concern for dry hair:** Repeated use on already-dry hair could exacerbate dryness. However, as a finishing spray applied to DRY, STYLED hair (not to wet/damp hair), the alcohol evaporates before it can significantly dehydrate the hair shaft. The contact time is fractions of a second. This is fundamentally different from an alcohol-based leave-in that sits on damp hair.

### 3C. Hydrofluorocarbon 152a (Position 2) — Aerosol Propellant

**Role:** Creates the spray mechanism. Evaporates instantly upon leaving the can. No functional role on hair.

**Environmental note:** HFC-152a has a global warming potential (GWP) of 124 — lower than older propellants (CFC-12: GWP 10,900) but still a greenhouse gas. The UK formulation uses dimethyl ether (GWP ~1) instead.

### 3D. Aminomethyl Propanol (Position 4) — pH Adjuster/Neutralizer

**Role:** Neutralizes the acidic Amphomer polymer to make it soluble in the alcohol base. Also adjusts pH for optimal film formation. Standard helper ingredient in hairspray formulations — no independent hair benefit.

### 3E. Triethyl Citrate (Position 6) — Plasticizer

**Role:** Softens the polymer film slightly to prevent it from being TOO brittle. Without a plasticizer, the Amphomer film would crack and flake off in chunks. Triethyl citrate provides just enough flexibility for the film to move with the hair without breaking apart, while maintaining the "freeze" hold.

**Key insight:** This is what makes Got2b "blasting freeze" different from a truly rigid spray — it has enough plasticizer to prevent flaking but not so much that it loses hold. The balance between Amphomer (rigid) and triethyl citrate (flexible) determines the final hold character.

### 3F. PEG/PPG-17/18 Dimethicone + PPG-12 Dimethicone (Positions 7, 10) — Silicone Conditioning

**Role:** Provide a small amount of conditioning/shine to the finished style. Prevent the polymer film from looking completely matte/dry. At positions 7 and 10 of 10, these are LOW concentration — just enough to add a touch of shine without compromising hold.

### 3G. AMP-Isostearoyl Hydrolyzed Wheat Protein (Position 8) — Conditioning Protein-Lipid Hybrid

**Verified:** "An aminomethyl propanol salt of the condensation product of isostearoyl chloride with Hydrolyzed Wheat Protein." Functions as hair conditioning agent. [EWG, CosIng]

**Role:** A lipophilic (fat-loving) protein derivative that provides conditioning within the hold film. The wheat protein is conjugated with isostearic acid, making it oil-soluble and able to integrate INTO the polymer film rather than sitting on top of it. This slightly softens the feel of the finished style without reducing hold.

**Position significance:** Position 8 of 10 = trace inclusion. Enough to slightly soften the feel, not enough to meaningfully condition or repair.

## 4. Delivery Format Validation

| Factor | Assessment | Impact |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Format | Aerosol hairspray (pressurized can) | Instant, even distribution; fine mist |
| Application state | DRY, styled hair (finishing step) | Applied AFTER styling is complete |
| Carrier system | Alcohol denat. + HFC-152a propellant | Flash evaporation = instant polymer deposit |
| Film character | Rigid with slight flexibility (Amphomer + triethyl citrate) | Holds style without flaking |
| Hold duration | "Until your next shampoo" / up to 72 hours (ASDA UK) | Survives sleep, humidity, wind |
| Removal | Water-soluble polymer — washes out with shampoo | No buildup concern; clean removal |
| Buildup risk | NONE — the polymer dissolves in water during washing | No clarifying needed |

**Delivery verdict:** The aerosol format is optimal for a finishing spray. The fine mist provides even coverage without disturbing the styled hair. The instant evaporation means the hold sets immediately — no waiting for product to dry. The water-soluble polymer means zero buildup concern despite the extreme hold.

**Critical distinction from gels:** Gels are applied to WET hair and define curl pattern as hair dries. This spray is applied to DRY hair and locks in whatever shape already exists. They serve completely different roles in the styling process.

## 8. Practical Education Section (for in-app use)

### Why you're using this

This is your "lock it in" spray — the final step after you've styled your hair exactly how you want it. It deposits a rigid, humidity-resistant film that holds everything in place until you wash it out. Think of it as hairspray, but specifically engineered for maximum hold that survives humidity, wind, and sleep.

It's NOT for curl days (that's what your gel does). It's for blowout days, updos, events, or any time you want a style to stay exactly where you put it.

### How to know it's working

- Your blowout stays smooth and in place all day, even in humidity
- Updos don't fall or loosen over the course of an evening
- Flyaways stay put after you smooth them down
- Your style looks the same at 10pm as it did at 8am
- Hair doesn't feel crunchy or flaky (the plasticizer prevents that)

### How to know it's NOT working

- Style falls or loosens within hours → you may not be using enough (thick/coarse hair needs more coverage — spray in sections, not just the surface)
- White residue or flaking → you're holding the can too close. Spray from 10-12 inches away for even distribution
- Hair feels sticky or wet → you're over-applying in one spot. Use quick, sweeping passes
- Style doesn't hold in extreme humidity → add a second pass after the first dries (30 seconds between coats)

### Why this over alternatives

- **vs. L'Oréal Elnett ($12-15/8oz):** Same Amphomer polymer technology, 3x the price. Elnett has a slightly softer hold (more plasticizer) which some prefer, but for maximum hold Got2b wins.
- **vs. Kenra Volume Spray 25 ($20+/10oz):** Professional-grade, similar hold. 4x the price for marginally better fragrance and packaging. The polymer technology is the same.
- **vs. drugstore "flexible hold" sprays:** Different category entirely. Flexible hold sprays use softer polymers (PVP/VA) that allow movement. Got2b is for when you want ZERO movement.
- **vs. NYM Curl Talk Gel:** Different tool entirely. Gel defines curls on wet hair. This locks in styles on dry hair. They're complementary, not competing.

### When to reach for something else

- **Curl definition days (air-dry)** → NYM Curl Talk Gel. The freeze spray would make curls crunchy and immovable — the opposite of what you want.
- **Soft, touchable hold** → A flexible hold spray or mousse. Got2b is for rigid hold.
- **Heat protection** → Marc Anthony Shield or Diamond Sleek BEFORE blow-drying. Got2b goes on AFTER.
- **Everyday light hold** → This is overkill for a casual day. Save it for when you need the style to survive hours of activity.

### If you're shopping for a replacement

Key things to look for:
- **Amphomer** (Octylacrylamide/Acrylates/Butylaminoethyl Methacrylate Copolymer) — this is the gold-standard rigid hold polymer
- **Aerosol format** for even distribution without disturbing the style
- **"Maximum hold" or "freeze hold" designation** — anything labeled "flexible" or "touchable" is a different category
- **Triethyl citrate or similar plasticizer** — prevents flaking while maintaining hold

What makes Got2b good value:
- $0.54-0.66/fl oz for a 12 oz can — one of the cheapest products in the inventory
- 10-ingredient formula (fewer potential irritants)
- Decades of market presence (proven, stable formulation)
- Water-soluble polymer (washes out cleanly, no buildup)

Red flags in alternatives:
- "Extreme hold" sprays without Amphomer → may use inferior polymers that flake or don't resist humidity
- Very cheap sprays with heavy fragrance → fragrance at high concentration can irritate scalp
- Non-aerosol "freeze sprays" → pump sprays don't atomize as finely, leading to uneven application
