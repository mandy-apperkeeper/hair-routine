## 1. Executive Summary

The Garnier Fructis Hair Filler Color Repair Conditioner is a remarkably clean 19-ingredient formula built on the same cetearyl alcohol + behentrimonium chloride + amodimethicone architecture as the L'Oréal EverPure Bond Repair Conditioner. Both products share the same parent company (L'Oréal) and the same core platform. The Garnier formula matches EverPure on every functional axis and exceeds it on three: higher amodimethicone concentration (position 3 vs 4), inclusion of a pseudoceramide (2-Oleamido-1,3-Octadecanediol) for cuticle lipid repair, and better value per ounce (~$9.99/10.1oz vs ~$9.99/6.8oz). Both use exclusively selective silicone (no dimethicone/dimethiconol), both provide citric acid + arginine bond support, and both use the same Trideceth-6 + Cetrimonium Chloride emulsifier system.

**Verdict:** Primary Rotation — co-primary with EverPure. For new purchases, Garnier is the recommended choice (same mechanism, additional pseudoceramide, 49% more product per dollar). For existing EverPure bottles, continue using — they are functionally equivalent.

## 3. Mechanism Assessment

### 3A. Cetearyl Alcohol — Structural Base

**Position:** 2 (very high concentration)

Same role as in EverPure: forms the lamellar gel network with behentrimonium chloride, provides slip, lubrication, and the vehicle for amodimethicone delivery. Position 2 means high concentration — appropriate for coarse, thick hair that needs substantial conditioning.

**Assessment:** Standard and effective. Identical role and position to EverPure.

### 3B. Amodimethicone — Selective Silicone Conditioning

**Position:** 3 (HIGHER than EverPure's position 4)

This is the key active ingredient. Amodimethicone selectively binds to damaged areas of the hair via protonated amine groups attracted to negatively charged damaged cuticle. It self-limits via like-charge repulsion — once a monolayer deposits, additional molecules are repelled.

**Verified:** Per Lab Muffin (Wong, April 2024): Amodimethicone selectively binds to damaged areas and self-limits via like-charge repulsion. ([Lab Muffin, April 2024](https://labmuffin.com/amodimethicone-my-new-favourite-hair-ingredient/))

**Position significance:** At position 3 (after water and cetearyl alcohol), amodimethicone is the THIRD ingredient by concentration. In EverPure, it's at position 4 (after behentrimonium chloride). This means Garnier has a slightly higher amodimethicone-to-quat ratio. In practice, both are high-concentration — the difference is modest but real.

**Delivery system:** Trideceth-6 (position 8) + Cetrimonium Chloride (position 12) — identical emulsifier package to EverPure. The cationic emulsion breaks during rinsing, releasing amodimethicone to deposit on hair.

**Assessment:** Optimal delivery scenario. High concentration + conditioner contact time (1-2 min) + selective deposition = maximum benefit for mixed-damage hair (TE regrowth + weathered cuticle).

### 3C. Behentrimonium Chloride — Primary Conditioning Agent

**Position:** 4 (high concentration — slightly lower position than EverPure's position 3)

**Verified:** Per HairAide (2025): Behentrimonium Chloride is a C22 long-chain cationic quaternary ammonium compound providing "exceptional slip, softness, and detangling." Best for curly hair (3A-4C), coarse hair, damaged hair. ([HairAide, 2025](https://hairaide.com/ingredients/behentrimonium-chloride/))

The C22 carbon chain lies flat along the hair shaft after charge-attraction deposition, creating a lubricating layer that reduces inter-strand friction. This is the gold standard detangling agent for coarse, curly hair.

**Position note:** In Garnier, behentrimonium chloride is at position 4 (after amodimethicone). In EverPure, it's at position 3 (before amodimethicone). This means Garnier prioritizes silicone conditioning slightly over quat conditioning, while EverPure does the reverse. Both are high-concentration — the functional difference is negligible.

**Assessment:** Same mechanism, same efficacy as EverPure. The position swap (3↔4) between amodimethicone and behentrimonium chloride doesn't meaningfully change performance.

### 3D. Arginine — Protein Support

**Position:** 10 (higher than EverPure's position 12)

Arginine is a cationic amino acid — one of the primary building blocks of hair keratin. Its positive charge makes it substantive to negatively charged damaged hair. It works synergistically with citric acid.

**Verified:** Per INCIDecoder: Arginine is a skin-identical ingredient with conditioning properties. ([INCIDecoder](https://incidecoder.com/ingredients/arginine))

**Position significance:** At position 10 (vs EverPure's 12), Garnier has slightly more arginine. Still a supporting ingredient at this concentration, but marginally more present.

### 3E. Citric Acid — pH Maintenance and Bond Support

**Position:** 11 (higher than EverPure's position 13)

Maintains acidic pH (keeps cuticle closed), chelates mineral deposits, and provides non-covalent protein network reinforcement.

**Verified:** Per PMID 39757966 (Jan 2026): Citric acid reinforces non-covalent protein networks in hair. ([PubMed, Jan 2026](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39757966/))

**Assessment:** Same mechanism as EverPure, slightly higher concentration (position 11 vs 13).

### 3F. 2-Oleamido-1,3-Octadecanediol — Pseudoceramide (UNIQUE TO GARNIER)

**Position:** 17
**CAS:** 54422-45-6
**Chemical name:** 2-(9-(Z)-Octadecenylamido)-1,3-Octadecanediol

This is the key differentiator from EverPure. It is a synthetic analog of naturally occurring ceramide-2, one of the three primary lipid types found in the hair cuticle (along with 18-MEA and cholesterol).

**Verified:** Per INCIDecoder: Listed as a "skin conditioning" agent. Chemical name confirms it's a ceramide-2 structural analog. ([INCIDecoder](https://incidecoder.com/ingredients/2-oleamido-1-3-octadecanediol))

**Verified:** Per Long Hair Community forums (2014, confirmed across multiple sources): "L'Oréal label [ceramide-2] as '2-oleamido-1,3-octadecanediol'" — this is L'Oréal's standard INCI name for their pseudoceramide ingredient. ([Long Hair Community](https://forums.longhaircommunity.com/showthread.php?t=120288))

**Verified:** Per Cosmetics & Toiletries (2025): "Along with 18 methyl eicosanoic acids and cholesterols, ceramides are one of three types of lipids found inside the cuticle of human hair, functioning as a glue to keep the scales in place. In damaged hair, however, ceramides are often significantly decreased or missing." A ceramide-2 analog was developed for application in hair care to recover lost barrier functions. ([Cosmetics & Toiletries, 2025](https://www.cosmeticsandtoiletries.com/research/literature-data/article/21837070/restoring-hair-and-scalp-health-ceramide2-analog-for-conditioning-and-barrier-benefits))

**Verified:** Per HairFolli (2025): "Ceramides are lipids naturally found in the hair cuticle. They seal the outer layer of each strand, lock in moisture, and reduce breakage. A 2018 study published in Cosmetics and Toiletries evaluated a ceramide-2 analogue in both shampoo and conditioner formulas. Applied to damaged hair samples, it improved combability, flattened lifted cuticle scales, and increased strand strength." ([HairFolli, 2025](https://hairfolli.com/blogs/news/are-ceramides-good-for-hair))

**Mechanism:** Ceramides function as intercellular cement between cuticle scales. When hair is damaged (chemical processing, heat, UV, mechanical stress), ceramide levels decrease, causing cuticle scales to lift. This leads to porosity, moisture loss, frizz, and progressive cortex damage. Topically applied pseudoceramide fills the gaps in the depleted lipid layer, helping cuticle scales lie flat again.

**Delivery format validation:** In a rinse-off conditioner with 1-2 minutes contact time, the pseudoceramide has opportunity to deposit in the intercellular spaces of the cuticle. Its lipophilic nature means it has affinity for the existing lipid matrix. However, at position 17 (out of 19 ingredients), the concentration is low. This is a supporting benefit, not a primary mechanism.

**Practical significance for Mandy's hair:** Coarse hair with weathered cuticle (from TE recovery period + environmental damage) has depleted ceramide levels. Even at low concentration, pseudoceramide provides a benefit that EverPure's current formula simply doesn't offer. It's additive to the amodimethicone conditioning — amodimethicone coats the surface, while pseudoceramide fills gaps between cuticle scales.

**Assessment:** Genuine differentiator. Low concentration limits the magnitude of benefit, but the mechanism is sound and the ingredient addresses a real gap (cuticle lipid depletion) that amodimethicone alone doesn't fix.

### 3G. Argania Spinosa Kernel Oil (Argan Oil)

**Position:** 16

Argan oil is rich in oleic acid (43-49%), linoleic acid (29-36%), and vitamin E (tocopherols). It functions primarily as a surface emollient in this formula.

**Verified:** Per Cleveland Clinic (2022): "The fatty acids in [argan oil], like linoleic and oleic acids, are very hydrating. It also contains vitamin E and antioxidants that can prevent breakage and damage from free radicals." ([Cleveland Clinic, 2022](https://health.clevelandclinic.org/argan-oil-benefits/))

**Important context (established decision):** Argan oil does NOT penetrate the hair cortex — it stays in the outer ~5µm (surface/cuticle only). This is different from coconut oil, which penetrates due to lauric acid's protein affinity. In a rinse-off conditioner at position 16, argan oil provides modest surface emolliency during the contact period. Some will deposit on the cuticle surface during rinsing.

**Verified:** Per Fashion and Textiles (2022): Argan oil in rinse-off conditioner format was effective in "protecting the tensile properties of bleached hair" and "retaining the color of bleached hair." ([Fashion and Textiles, 2022](https://fashionandtextiles.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s40691-021-00282-5))

**Assessment:** Minor supporting ingredient at this position. Provides surface emolliency and antioxidant protection. Not a primary mechanism driver, but a legitimate addition that EverPure lacks.

### 3H. Chlorhexidine Dihydrochloride — Preservative

**Position:** 13

This is the preservative that differs from EverPure (which uses phenoxyethanol + benzoic acid). Chlorhexidine is a broad-spectrum antimicrobial that disrupts microbial cell membranes.

**Verified:** Per Moumoujus: "Chlorhexidine Dihydrochloride is a powerful cationic antimicrobial agent with broad-spectrum activity against bacteria, fungi, and some viruses. CIR Expert Panel recommends a maximum concentration of 0.16% for cosmetic products." Effective range: 0.05-0.16%. ([Moumoujus](https://moumoujus.com/ingredients/chlorhexidine-dihydrochloride/))

**Verified:** Per ResearchGate/PMC (2014): "According to the European Cosmetic Directive, chlorhexidine is allowed in cosmetic products in a concentration of up to 0.3%." ([PMC4128301](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4128301/))

**Verified:** Per HairAide (2025): "Safe at low cosmetic concentrations. Can cause tooth staining in oral products and rare allergic reactions including anaphylaxis in medical settings. Scalp product concentrations are much lower than medical use." ([HairAide, 2025](https://hairaide.com/ingredients/chlorhexidine/))

**Safety assessment:** At position 13 (out of 19), the concentration is well below the 0.16% CIR maximum. In a rinse-off conditioner applied to hair (not scalp), exposure is minimal. The EU allows up to 0.3% in cosmetics. This is a standard preservative choice — different from EverPure's benzoic acid but equally safe at cosmetic concentrations.

**CG Method note:** HairAide flags chlorhexidine as "CG Avoid" — but this is irrelevant for Mandy's routine, which uses silicones (amodimethicone) and is not CG-method.

**Potential concern:** EWG flags "High Allergies & Immunotoxicity" for this product, driven primarily by chlorhexidine and fragrance components. However, EWG's scoring is conservative and based on worst-case medical-setting data. At rinse-off cosmetic concentrations, the risk is negligible for non-sensitized individuals.

**Assessment:** Safe preservative at this concentration in a rinse-off product. No practical concern for Mandy's use case. If she ever develops scalp sensitivity, this would be one ingredient to investigate — but that's true of any preservative system.

### 3I. Other Ingredients

- **Cetyl Esters** (position 5) — emollient/thickener. Provides additional slip and body to the formula.
- **Isopropyl Alcohol** (position 7) — solvent for the amodimethicone emulsion system. Evaporates. Not drying at this concentration in a rinse-off product.
- **Phenoxyethanol** (position 9) — primary preservative. Safe, industry standard.
- **Linalool, Limonene, Citronellol** (positions 14, 15, 19) — fragrance components. Required to be listed individually in EU/international labeling.

## 8. Practical Education Section (for in-app use)

### Why you're using this

This is your every-wash conditioner — functionally identical to the EverPure you already know, made by the same parent company (L'Oréal owns Garnier). Its amodimethicone seeks out the most damaged parts of your hair and deposits a protective film there. The behentrimonium chloride provides the slip you need to detangle thick, wet curls without snapping strands. The pseudoceramide (listed as 2-Oleamido-1,3-Octadecanediol) fills gaps in your cuticle's natural lipid layer — think of it as patching the mortar between bricks.

### How to know it's working

- Wet hair detangles easily during and after application
- Hair feels smooth and slippery when rinsing — not squeaky, not slimy
- Dried hair has shine and reduced frizz
- You don't need to clarify frequently (amodimethicone self-limits)
- Curl definition holds — curls spring back rather than being weighed down
- Over time (weeks): hair feels less porous, holds moisture better between washes (pseudoceramide cumulative effect)

### How to know it's NOT working

- Hair feels heavy, limp, or greasy after drying → using too much, or hair isn't damaged enough for this level of conditioning (unlikely for your profile)
- Hair still tangles badly → try leaving on longer (2-3 min), or apply more generously to ends
- No difference from a cheaper conditioner → hair may have improved (less damaged = less selective deposition benefit). Good news.
- Scalp feels coated or itchy → applying too close to roots. Focus on mid-lengths and ends.
- Any new scalp irritation → chlorhexidine sensitivity (rare). Switch back to EverPure which uses benzoic acid instead.

### Why this over alternatives

- **vs. EverPure Bond Repair:** Same core formula. Garnier adds pseudoceramide and gives you 49% more product for the same price. If you're buying new, this is the better deal.
- **vs. Dove conditioners:** Dove uses dimethiconol (non-selective, builds up). This uses amodimethicone only (selective, self-limiting). No contest.
- **vs. silicone-free conditioners:** For coarse, damaged hair, silicone-free often doesn't provide enough smoothing. Amodimethicone gives silicone benefits without buildup.
- **vs. heavy mask-type conditioners:** Those are for weekly deep conditioning. This is gentle enough for every wash.

### When to reach for something else

- **Deep conditioning day** (weekly) → mask or deep conditioner with heat cap (10+ min)
- **Protein treatment day** → Olaplex 3 or protein treatment, then follow with this to seal
- **Post-clarifying** → This conditioner is perfect after clarifying — stripped hair attracts more amodimethicone
- **If hair feels over-conditioned** → Skip conditioner for one wash (rare with amodimethicone)

### If you're shopping for a replacement

Key things to look for:
- **Amodimethicone** (not dimethicone/dimethiconol) in the top 5 ingredients
- **Behentrimonium chloride** (C22 chain) for coarse hair
- **Cetearyl alcohol** high in the list
- **No sulfates** in the conditioner
- **Acidic pH** (citric acid or similar)
- **Bonus:** pseudoceramide or ceramide-2 analog (2-Oleamido-1,3-Octadecanediol)

Red flags to avoid:
- Dimethicone or Dimethiconol as primary silicone (buildup)
- DMDM Hydantoin (formaldehyde releaser)
- Very short ingredient list with no silicone (insufficient smoothing for coarse hair)
- Isopropyl Myristate high in list (comedogenic)
