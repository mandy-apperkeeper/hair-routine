## Key Mechanisms Found

### 1. Amodimethicone (Position 4)
- **Verified:** Selective silicone that binds to damaged cuticle areas via protonated amine groups. Self-limits via like-charge repulsion. [Lab Muffin, April 2024; also verified in garnier-color-repair-cond.md]
- **Position:** 4th ingredient (after water, cetearyl alcohol, behentrimonium chloride). Same position as in Garnier conditioner (position 3 in Garnier, but Garnier has amodimethicone before behentrimonium chloride — functionally equivalent high concentration in both).
- **Delivery system:** Trideceth-6 (pos 11) + Cetrimonium Chloride (pos 15) — same emulsifier package as Garnier.
- **Conclusion:** SAME primary active as Garnier conditioner. Same mechanism, same delivery system.

### 2. Hydroxypropyltrimonium Hydrolyzed Wheat Protein (Position 19)
- **Verified:** Cationic (quaternized) hydrolyzed wheat protein. The quaternization adds positive charge, increasing substantivity to negatively-charged damaged hair. [INCIDecoder: "antistatic, hair conditioning, skin conditioning"; CIR Panel 2018: safe at ≤3,500 Da average MW]
- **Mechanism:** Small peptide fragments (≤3,500 Da per CIR safety panel) penetrate into the hair cortex to fill gaps in damaged protein structures. Larger fractions coat the cuticle surface. The cationic charge enhances binding to damaged areas. [HairAide, 2025; BiologyInsights, 2025]
- **Penetration evidence:** Research on hydrolyzed keratins (analogous mechanism) shows low MW peptides (<1,000 Da) penetrate deep into cortex; mid MW (1,000-10,000 Da) penetrate outer cortex; high MW (>10,000 Da) coat surface only. [Malinauskyte et al., Int J Cosmet Sci, 2020]
- **Elasticity claim:** "Can increase hair elasticity by roughly 35%" [BiologyInsights citing Cosmetics & Toiletries Magazine — Unverified primary source, treat as indicative]
- **Position significance:** Position 19 of 33 = low concentration. Likely in the 0.1-0.5% range. Still functional for protein fill but not a high-dose protein treatment.
- **GARNIER COMPARISON:** Garnier conditioner does NOT contain any hydrolyzed protein. This is a genuine differentiator.

### 3. 2-Oleamido-1,3-Octadecanediol (Position 24)
- **Verified:** Synthetic pseudoceramide. Biomimetic lipid that integrates into intercellular lamellae. Acts as metabolic precursor converted to longer-chain ceramides. [Moumoujus; CIR: safe as used, max tested 0.2% leave-on, 0.7% rinse-off]
- **Effective concentration:** Min 0.05% for meaningful barrier restoration. Red flag below 0.01%. [Moumoujus]
- **Position significance:** Position 24 of 33 = very low concentration. In a rinse-off product, likely near the minimum effective threshold (0.05-0.1%). Functional but not a high-dose ceramide treatment.
- **GARNIER COMPARISON:** Garnier conditioner ALSO contains 2-Oleamido-1,3-Octadecanediol (position 17 of 19 — also low concentration). Both have it. NOT a differentiator.

### 4. Sweet Almond Oil (Position 13) + Sweet Almond Fruit Extract (Position 28)
- **Verified:** Prunus Amygdalus Dulcis Oil. Emollient, primarily oleic acid (62-86%) and linoleic acid (20-30%). [General cosmetic chemistry knowledge — Unverified specific percentages]
- **Penetration:** Unlike coconut oil (lauric acid, penetrates cortex), almond oil does NOT significantly penetrate the hair shaft. It coats the surface. [Rele & Mohile, 2003, J Cosmet Sci: only coconut oil reduced protein loss; sunflower and mineral oils did not. Almond oil behaves similarly to sunflower — surface coating only]
- **Role in this formula:** Surface emollient/lubricant. Adds slip and shine. Does not provide cortex-level benefit.
- **GARNIER COMPARISON:** Garnier has Argania Spinosa Kernel Oil (argan oil) at position 16. Both are surface-coating oils. Neither penetrates. Functionally equivalent role.

### 5. Behentrimonium Chloride (Position 3)
- Same C22 cationic quat as Garnier. Same detangling/conditioning mechanism. Same high concentration.

### 6. Candelilla Wax (Position 5)
- Thickening/structuring agent. Gives the "balm" its thick texture. Garnier doesn't have this — Garnier is a thinner conditioner format.
- **Significance for mask format:** The wax creates a thicker, more occlusive layer that may enhance dwell-time deposition of actives (amodimethicone, protein). This is a format advantage for deep conditioning.

### 7. Hydroxypropyl Guar (Position 12)
- Conditioning polymer, adds slip. Present in Elvive but not Garnier. Minor differentiator.

### 8. Magnesium Aluminum Silicate (Position 27)
- Clay-based thickener/stabilizer. Structural role only.

## Complementarity Verdict

**Question:** Does the Elvive provide complementary benefits despite shared amodimethicone?

**Answer: WEAK YES — but the complementarity is marginal, not strong.**

The only genuinely different mechanism is the hydrolyzed wheat protein (cortex penetration/protein fill). However:
1. It's at position 19 of 33 — very low concentration
2. In a rinse-off format (even with 3-5 min dwell time), protein deposition is limited
3. The primary active (amodimethicone) is identical in both products
4. The pseudoceramide is shared by both

**For the app's mechanism check:** The current binary check (does mask contain amodimethicone? → yes → replaces conditioner) is CORRECT for this product pair. The wheat protein at position 19 does not provide enough differentiation to justify using both products in the same wash. The amodimethicone in the Elvive already provides the cuticle conditioning that the Garnier provides — adding Garnier after Elvive would be redundant silicone layering with no additional benefit.

**When Elvive IS complementary:** If paired with a conditioner that does NOT contain amodimethicone (e.g., a pure protein conditioner or a humectant-only conditioner), the Elvive's silicone + protein combination would be genuinely complementary.
