## 1. Executive Summary

The OGX Argan Oil of Morocco is functionally identical to the OGX Coconut Miracle Oil — a volatile silicone serum (cyclopentasiloxane + cyclotetrasiloxane + dimethiconol) with a trace amount of argan oil at position 4-5. The "penetrating oil" name is equally misleading here: argan oil does NOT penetrate the hair cortex (it lacks the lauric acid that gives coconut oil its unique penetration ability). The product is a silicone finishing serum with argan oil as a marketing ingredient.

**Verdict:** Use-Up tier is correct. This is the same product as the OGX Coconut Oil with a different trace oil and different fragrance. Same mechanism (silicone coating), same limitations (no penetration, no repair, no conditioning beyond surface smoothing), same redundancy with Dove 10-in-1 and L'Oréal 21-in-1. Do not repurchase.

## 3. Mechanism Assessment

### What This Product Actually Does

**Identical mechanism to OGX Coconut Oil:** Volatile silicone carrier evaporates → dimethiconol film remains → shine + frizz smoothing.

The only functional difference from the Coconut Oil version is the trace oil:
- **Coconut oil** (in the other product) — has demonstrated cortex penetration ability at high concentrations (pure application). At 1-3% in silicone, this ability is negligible.
- **Argan oil** (in this product) — does NOT penetrate the hair cortex at ANY concentration. Research shows argan oil stays in the outer 5µm of the hair fiber (the cuticle surface). It's an excellent surface emollient but not a penetrating oil.

This is established in the session-context.md: "Argan oil does NOT penetrate (stays in outer 5µm)."

**What argan oil actually provides at this concentration:**
- Surface lubrication (oleic acid + linoleic acid)
- Mild antioxidant protection (tocopherols naturally present in argan oil)
- Pleasant scent contribution

At 1-3% concentration in a silicone matrix, these benefits are minimal — the dimethiconol film dominates the surface properties.

### What it does NOT do:
- Does NOT penetrate hair (argan oil physically cannot, and the concentration is too low regardless)
- Does NOT "renew" hair (marketing claim with no mechanism)
- Does NOT repair damage
- Does NOT provide heat protection
- Does NOT condition (no cationic agents)

## 5. Efficacy for Hair Profile (2C-3A Coarse/Thick/Dry)

### Strengths:
Identical to OGX Coconut Oil — shine, frizz smoothing, lightweight finish. See that deep dive for details.

### Weaknesses:
Identical to OGX Coconut Oil — no moisture, no protein, buildup risk, redundant with better products. Plus one additional weakness:

- **Argan oil is LESS useful than coconut oil for this hair type.** Even at trace concentrations, coconut oil has some affinity for hair proteins (lauric acid). Argan oil has zero penetration ability. If forced to choose between the two OGX oils, the coconut version is marginally better for coarse/dry hair — but neither is worth repurchasing.

### Net assessment:
Same as OGX Coconut Oil. Functional as a finishing serum, redundant in the current inventory, not worth repurchasing.
