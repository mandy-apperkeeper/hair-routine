## Key Mechanisms Found

### 1. Citric Acid (Position 6) — PRIMARY ACTIVE for hair repair
- **Verified:** Peer-reviewed study (2024/2025): "CA treatment demonstrably enhances the structural integrity of chemically treated hair. DSC revealed increased crosslinking density of keratin-associated proteins (KAPs) in the hair cortex, evidenced by a 6.7% to 15.0% increase in denaturation temperature." [PubMed 39757966, Jan 2025]
- **Mechanism:** Citric acid forms ester crosslinks between hydroxyl groups on keratin-associated proteins (KAPs) in the cortex. This creates new covalent bonds that reinforce the internal structure — analogous to (but different from) Olaplex's bis-aminopropyl diglycol dimaleate which reforms disulfide bonds.
- **Position significance:** Position 6 = HIGH concentration. This is not a pH adjuster at trace levels — it's a functional active. The sodium citrate (position 8) acts as a buffer to maintain the acidic pH needed for crosslinking without over-acidifying.
- **Pre-shampoo context:** Applied before shampoo means the citric acid has extended contact time (5-10 min per user reports) on wet hair before being rinsed. The acidic pH also closes the cuticle, which may help retain the crosslinks formed during dwell time.
- **Comparison to Olaplex:** Different mechanism. Olaplex reforms broken disulfide bonds (S-S). Citric acid creates NEW ester crosslinks between KAPs. Both strengthen the cortex but via different chemical pathways. They are complementary, not redundant.

### 2. Palmitoyl Tripeptide-1 + Palmitoyl Tetrapeptide-7 (Positions 21-22) — "Matrixyl 3000"
- **Verified:** These two peptides together = Matrixyl 3000, primarily known as a skin anti-aging complex. [Typology UK, 2025]
- **Skin mechanism (verified):** Palmitoyl Tripeptide-1 (GHK fragment) stimulates collagen synthesis (types I, III, IV, VII) and fibronectin. Palmitoyl Tetrapeptide-7 (GQPR) is a signaling peptide involved in dermal homeostasis. [Typology UK citing TIAN J. et al. 2020, WANG Y. et al. 2024]
- **Hair mechanism (Inferred):** Garnier claims these peptides "repair and strengthen broken bonds deep in the cortex" and "fill hair with strength 7 layers deep." However, NO peer-reviewed studies were found specifically demonstrating Matrixyl 3000's efficacy on hair fiber (as opposed to skin/scalp). The peptides are lipophilic (palmitoylated) which could theoretically aid penetration into the hair shaft, but this is unverified for hair specifically.
- **Position significance:** Positions 21-22 of 23 = VERY low concentration. Likely in the 0.001-0.01% range. At these concentrations, the peptides may be more marketing than functional for hair fiber repair. However, even at low concentrations, signaling peptides can have effects on the scalp/follicle if they reach it.
- **Honest assessment:** The "15% concentrate of peptides, citric acid, and bond repair complex" marketing claim likely means 15% of a proprietary blend that CONTAINS these ingredients — not 15% peptides. The actual peptide concentration is almost certainly below 0.1%.

### 3. Behentrimonium Chloride (Position 4) — Conditioning/Detangling
- Same C22 cationic quat as in the Garnier conditioner and Elvive mask. Provides slip, detangling, and helps the treatment distribute evenly.
- **Pre-shampoo context:** The conditioning agents here serve a dual purpose: (1) protect hair during the subsequent shampoo step (reduce protein loss from surfactants), and (2) provide immediate detangling benefit.

### 4. Glycerin (Position 3) — Humectant
- High position = high concentration. Draws moisture into the hair shaft. In a pre-shampoo context, helps hydrate the cortex before the drying effects of shampoo.

### 5. Polyquaternium-10 (Position 12) — Film-forming conditioning polymer
- Deposits a thin cationic film on hair. Provides slip and reduces static. Helps the citric acid crosslinks survive the subsequent shampoo step by creating a protective layer.

### 6. Arginine (Position 19) — Amino acid
- Same as in the Garnier conditioner. Provides protein support and pH buffering. At position 19, low concentration.

### 7. Argania Spinosa Kernel Oil (Position 18) — Argan oil
- Surface emollient. Same as in the conditioner. Provides slip and shine.

| Garnier Pre-Shampoo | Citric acid ester crosslinks + peptides | Before shampoo | Creates NEW crosslinks (not reforming broken ones) |

## Contradictions

1. **"15% concentrate" claim is misleading.** The peptides are at positions 21-22 of 23 ingredients — clearly trace amounts. The "15%" likely refers to a proprietary blend percentage, not the concentration of any single active. The citric acid (position 6) is the real workhorse at meaningful concentration.
2. **Peptides for hair vs skin:** All peer-reviewed evidence for Matrixyl 3000 is on SKIN (collagen stimulation, wrinkle reduction). No published studies demonstrate these specific peptides repairing hair fiber structure. Garnier's "bond repair" claim for the peptides is extrapolated from skin science, not validated on hair.
3. **"7 layers deep" claim:** Hair fiber doesn't have 7 distinct layers in the way this marketing implies. The cortex has concentric layers of macrofibrils, but the "7 layers" framing is marketing, not anatomy.

---

## Gaps

- No peer-reviewed studies on Matrixyl 3000 specifically for hair fiber repair (only skin)

## Key Findings for Write Session

1. **Tier placement (Supporting) is CORRECT.** It's a genuinely useful product with a scientifically-grounded mechanism (citric acid crosslinking), but it's a treatment step, not a daily essential. Supporting Cast is appropriate.
2. **The citric acid is the real active, not the peptides.** Position 6 with extended dwell time = meaningful crosslinking. The peptides at positions 21-22 are likely cosmetic dusting for marketing purposes.
3. **Complementary to Olaplex 3** — different bond types (ester vs disulfide). Both can be used in the same routine.
4. **Complementary to the Garnier conditioner** — the pre-shampoo provides cortex crosslinking that the conditioner doesn't deliver (conditioner's citric acid is at position 11, lower concentration, shorter contact time).
5. **The "pre-shampoo treatment is marketing" established decision needs nuance.** The session-context says "Pre-shampoo treatment is marketing — bond repair works best after shampoo, sealed by conditioner." This is true for Olaplex-style disulfide repair. But citric acid crosslinking is a DIFFERENT mechanism that may actually benefit from pre-shampoo application (acidic pH closes cuticle, extended dwell time, crosslinks form before surfactant exposure). The established decision should be updated to distinguish between these two mechanisms.
6. **Excellent value** at $0.89-0.99/fl oz for a treatment that provides genuine cortex-level repair.
