# Phase 2: Formulation Position & Delivery Systems

## The Core Question

When does "has ingredient X" actually mean "delivers ingredient X effectively" vs "contains a negligible amount"?

---

## Rule 1: INCI List Position Matters — But Not Linearly

**The 1% line:** In the EU and US, ingredients above 1% concentration must be listed in descending order. Below 1%, they can be listed in any order. The practical challenge: you can't tell exactly where the 1% line falls without lab analysis.

**Heuristic for identifying the 1% line:**
- Preservatives (phenoxyethanol, methylisothiazolinone) are almost always at or below 1%
- Fragrance/parfum is typically 0.5-2% — often near the line
- If an active ingredient appears AFTER preservatives or fragrance, it's likely below 1%

**Application to Mandy's products:**

| Product | Amodimethicone Position | Assessment |
|---------|------------------------|------------|
| Garnier Filler Serum | High in list (before fragrance) | Functionally significant concentration |
| L'Oréal EverPure Bond Repair Conditioner | Listed as "bis-cetearyl amodimethicone" — mid-list | Moderate concentration, still effective (emulsion delivery helps) |
| L'Oréal 21-in-1 Leave-In | Mid-list, before fragrance | Effective — leave-in delivery means 100% stays on hair |
| Dove 10-in-1 Serum | Listed after several other actives | Lower concentration, but leave-on format compensates |

---

## Rule 2: Delivery Vehicle Changes Everything

The same ingredient at the same concentration performs differently depending on:

### Rinse-off vs Leave-on

**Rinse-off (conditioner, mask):** Only a fraction of the ingredient deposits onto hair. Contact time matters. For amodimethicone in a rinse-off conditioner, deposition depends on:
- Emulsion type (cationic emulsion deposits more than non-ionic)
- Contact time (3-5 minutes >> 30 seconds)
- Hair damage level (more damage = more negative charge = more deposition)
- Water temperature (warm water opens cuticle, allows more deposition)

**Leave-on (serum, spray, leave-in):** 100% of applied product stays on hair. Even a lower concentration ingredient in a leave-on can outperform a higher concentration in a rinse-off.

**Practical implication for Mandy:**
- The Dove 10-in-1 Serum (leave-on) with amodimethicone "buried in formula" may deliver MORE amodimethicone to hair than the L'Oréal conditioner (rinse-off) despite lower list position — because nothing gets rinsed away.
- The L'Oréal 21-in-1 Leave-In spray is the most efficient amodimethicone delivery vehicle in the collection for this reason.

### Emulsion Type for Silicones

Per Lab Muffin (Wong 2024) and the nanotribological study (LaTorre & Bhushan, J Vac Sci Technol A, 2005):

**Cationic emulsion (amodimethicone + cetrimonium chloride):** The cationic emulsifier helps the silicone deposit onto hair. This is the gold standard for rinse-off amodimethicone delivery.

**Non-ionic emulsion:** Silicone deposits less efficiently. Still works, but requires longer contact time.

**Pre-emulsified (microemulsion):** Very fine droplets, deposits uniformly but thinly. Good for fine hair, less impactful for coarse hair that needs more coverage.

**Application to Mandy's products:**
- L'Oréal EverPure Bond Repair Conditioner uses a cationic emulsion system — good delivery
- Garnier Filler Serum is a leave-on serum — direct application, no emulsion concerns

## Rule 3: Binary vs Concentration-Dependent Ingredients

Some ingredients work on a "present or not" basis. Others need a minimum concentration to function.

### Binary (threshold: present at any meaningful amount)
- **Bis-aminopropyl diglycol dimaleate (Olaplex):** Works at very low concentrations because it's a catalyst-like molecule that reconnects bonds. Olaplex 3 is effective despite the active being a small percentage.
- **Citric acid (pH adjustment):** Even small amounts shift pH enough to close cuticles. The effect is about the final formulation pH, not the citric acid concentration per se.
- **Preservatives:** Function at their minimum inhibitory concentration — binary effective/not.

### Concentration-Dependent (more = more effect, up to a plateau)
- **Amodimethicone:** More deposition = smoother hair, up to the self-limiting plateau. Products with higher concentrations deposit more per application.
- **Hydrolyzed proteins:** Penetration depth and strengthening effect scale with concentration and molecular weight distribution. The OGX pre-shampoo with proteins listed high is meaningfully different from a product with "hydrolyzed keratin" buried at the end.
- **Panthenol:** Penetration and humectant effect scale with concentration. Typically effective at 1-5% in formulations.
- **Coconut oil:** Penetration into cortex requires sufficient concentration and contact time. A trace amount in a rinse-off won't penetrate meaningfully.
- **PQ-69 (when purchased):** Film thickness and hold strength scale with concentration. Gel format (high concentration) >> spray format (low concentration).

### Plateau/Self-Limiting
- **Amodimethicone:** Self-limiting due to charge repulsion. After ~3-5 applications, deposition reaches equilibrium. Additional applications maintain but don't increase the film.
- **Cationic surfactants (conditioning):** Monolayer deposition — once the surface is covered, additional surfactant doesn't help. This is why "more conditioner" doesn't mean "more conditioning" past a point.

## Rule 4: Contact Time Rules

| Product Type | Typical Contact | Deposition Efficiency |
|-------------|----------------|----------------------|
| Shampoo | 1-2 min | Very low (surfactants compete with deposition) |
| Rinse-off conditioner | 1-5 min | Moderate (longer = better for silicones) |
| Deep mask | 5-20 min | High (extended contact allows more deposition) |
| Leave-in spray | Indefinite | Very high (nothing removed) |
| Leave-on serum | Indefinite | Very high |
| Pre-shampoo treatment | 10-30 min | High for penetrating ingredients (proteins, oils) |
| Lamellar rinse (Wonder Water) | 10-30 sec | High despite short time (lamellar structure deposits rapidly on contact) |

**Key insight for Mandy:** The 3-5 minute conditioner contact time recommended in the consultation is specifically to maximize amodimethicone deposition from rinse-off products. Rushing this step (30 seconds) significantly reduces the benefit.

## Decision Rules for the App's Recommendation Engine

Based on this research, the app should use these rules when selecting products:

1. **Leave-on > rinse-off** for the same ingredient at the same list position
2. **High list position + leave-on = maximum delivery** (Garnier Serum, L'Oréal 21-in-1)
3. **Contact time matters for rinse-offs** — always recommend 3-5 min for conditioners
4. **Volatile silicone products provide no lasting benefit** — deprioritize OGX oils
5. **Protein treatments must be pre-shampoo format** with extended contact to penetrate
6. **Olaplex works regardless of concentration** — binary mechanism
7. **Amodimethicone is self-limiting** — no need to "rotate off" it, no buildup concern
8. **Lamellar products (Wonder Water) are uniquely efficient** — rapid deposition despite short contact
