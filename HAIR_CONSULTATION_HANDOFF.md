## Key Findings This Session

### Product Audit Results
- **Best products (amodimethicone-based, targeted cuticle repair):** Garnier Hair Filler conditioner/serum, L'Oréal EverPure Bond Repair conditioner, L'Oréal 21-in-1 leave-in, Dove 10-in-1 serum (leave-on)
- **Generic smoothing (temporary, non-targeted):** All Dove conditioners/masks, OGX oils, OGX Moroccan conditioner
- **The Glossing Lamination Mask is NOT a conditioning mask** — it's a clarifying/exfoliating treatment (surfactants + glycolic acid). Use as occasional reset, not for softness.
- **NOTHING in the entire collection has a strong-hold film-forming polymer** — no PQ-69, no VP/VA copolymer. This is the missing humidity barrier.

### The Diagnosis
1. **Frizz (still significant):** Missing hold polymer. No product creates a humidity barrier. Recommended: Not Your Mother's Curl Talk Flash Freeze Gel (~$8, PQ-69 based).
2. **Bangs/face-framing sticking out:** Telogen effluvium regrowth. Short fine hairs at hairline/temples that are too short to lie flat. Not a product problem — time (12-18 months) is the primary fix. Light pomade/edge control or strategic blow-drying direction can help cosmetically.
3. **Wants more softness:** On the right trajectory but only ~3 weeks in. Amodimethicone builds cumulatively. Recommendation: use Garnier or L'Oréal conditioner EVERY wash (stop alternating with Dove). Leave conditioner on 3-5 minutes. Section hair for even distribution.

### Verified Science (New This Session)
- Coarse hair: 80-120+ μm diameter, 10-12 cuticle layers (vs 5-7 fine), larger cortex = more water exchange surface area
- Amodimethicone: selectively binds damaged (negatively charged) hair via amine groups, self-limiting (won't build up), verified via Lab Muffin/Wong 2024
- 18-MEA lipid layer: cannot regenerate once damaged, must be replaced (amodimethicone or synthetic 18-MEA like Croda Cutissential)
- PQ-69: specifically engineered for humidity resistance, forms rigid film that maintains integrity in moisture
- L'Oréal 8 Second Wonder Water: IS conditioning (cationic surfactant deposition), spray bottle is fine (lamellar structure likely forms on contact with wet hair, not pre-formed in bottle)
- L'Oréal Glossing Lamination Mask: IS clarifying (contains surfactants + glycolic acid AHA), not conditioning despite "mask" name
- "Moisture" in hair care is marketing — hair with more water feels rougher. "Moisturized" feeling = smooth surface coating.
- NIMS/L'Oréal 2023: PVA/cellulose microcrystal shape-memory polymer maintains curl at 80% humidity for 6+ hours (not in consumer products yet)
