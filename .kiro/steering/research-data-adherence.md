# Research Data Adherence — Hair Routine Project

**Extends:** Global Data Adherence rules in `deep-dive-auto.md`. This file adds hair-routine-specific tier criteria.

## Core Rule

Product tier placement, app recommendations, and compensation logic are driven ONLY by verified evidence. This applies to all research output and any resulting app changes.

## What Counts as Evidence for Changing Product Placement

A product's tier can only change if ALL of the following are met:

1. **Specific mechanism identified** — name the ingredient, its molecular behavior, and why it matters for this hair profile (2C-3A, coarse, thick, dry, TE regrowth)
2. **Sourced to peer-reviewed or expert-chemist literature** — not brand marketing, not influencer claims, not "common knowledge"
3. **Quantified or comparative** — "better" means better than what, by what measure, under what conditions
4. **Delivery-format validated** — a beneficial ingredient in a rinse-off format at low concentration is NOT equivalent to the same ingredient in a leave-on at high concentration. Account for actual delivery.
5. **Not contradicted by existing verified findings** — if the Phase 1-5 research already addressed this claim, the new evidence must be stronger, not just different

## What Does NOT Count

- "Could theoretically help" — inference without supporting data
- Brand claims or marketing copy (Dove's own "repair technology" language)
- Anecdotal reports without mechanism explanation
- Training data claims without live-verified sources
- "Some people find..." without controlled comparison

## Applying to the Dove Deep Dive Specifically

The current placement (use-up tier) is based on:
- Dimethiconol/silsesquioxane = non-selective, non-cumulative, temporary
- No unique function not better served by other products in inventory
- Interaction penalty: reduces subsequent product deposition

To change this placement, research must find evidence that:
- The non-selective coating provides a SPECIFIC benefit for coarse/2C-3A hair that amodimethicone does NOT
- OR SH-Polypeptide-121 delivers meaningful benefit despite low concentration + rinse-off format
- OR the full formulation (not just hero ingredients) provides something the primary-tier products lack

If the deep dive confirms the current placement, that's a valid and useful outcome. The goal is accuracy, not rehabilitation.

## After Research Completes

Any proposed tier change must be presented as:
- "Evidence found: [specific claim] from [source, date]. This suggests [specific change] because [mechanism]. Confidence: [high/medium/low]."

No tier changes without this format. No "based on the research, it seems like maybe..."
